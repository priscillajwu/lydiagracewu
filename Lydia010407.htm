January 4, 2007, Thursday

 

Eleven days before Christmas, the Thailand mission team from our church left for Thailand to share the gospel in the remote mountain area…

We received this email from one of the team leaders two days ago…

 

 

 

Subject : Lydia's mission work

 

Dear Wu Family,

Our Thailand Team just got back yesterday.  I wanted to share with you how Lydia really was a missionary with us there. 

Though she could not be there in person, her witness was carried miles over seas.  We gave the bracelets to the Christian children in the local missionary’s hostel.  We also shared most of the PowerPoint with the students there.  We encouraged them to be strong for Jesus even when they are sick.  Jesus is always with us and our light should shine for him in all circumstances.  Elder UR shared this with the students. 

Death is not something foreign to these children.  Many of them have family members that have gone on.  Some do not have parents…and others have lost many siblings.  We met a family who lost every child they have had...recently they lost a 10 year-old boy.  The local missionary had to bury him.  This family also has a hard situation because the wife is Christian and the husband is not.  He says that Jesus made their sons die...and that it is punishment for not worshiping demons.  This has left the wife questioning and fearful.  Her husband continues to deny her emotional support.  She also feels guilty for their sons' deaths.  Please pray for this woman!

Lydia's story is powerful because many people can relate to it.  Many struggle…but all can find the ultimate cure in Jesus Christ. 

Even the missionary and his wife were wearing the bracelets.  The children loved them and put them on right away.  All through the next few weeks, many of the kids never took off the red bracelet.  This will provide them with the chance to tell about Jesus at school.  This style bracelet is popular in Thailand also and I heard of several people asking where they got the bracelets from.  I am excited for this opportunity!

In the mountains we met a lady named Ruth.  Ruth is about 27 or so.  Her husband was just killed in a wreck 2 months before we came.  With shaking hands she grasped onto the missionary and in tears asked us to pray for her.  We went into her house and sat in a circle to pray for her.  She was a graduate of the missionary’s Bible school (and is a Christian).  Her little child on her back was sleeping as we prayed.  My heart broke for her.  Unable to care for her family anymore, her world is upside down.  We later found out that the child was not hers…the baby was an orphan whose mother was Ruth's friend.  The baby's mother had died and Ruth had seen this and adopted the child.  I wanted to give the world back to this woman.  I wanted to give her what she had lost.  But I couldn't….but I was wearing my Lydia bracelet.  It is a story different, yet similar to hers.  I thought of Lydia and ultimately Jesus ….so I removed my Lydia bracelet and was able to give it to her a few days after we prayer for her.  She looked me right in the eyes and smiled.  Though she doesn't know what the bracelet means directly….I think she still understood the message of Lydia's bracelet:

"I can't undo what has been done…but God is right here with you."

Thank you, Lydia…for the chance to share the hope you knew Jesus gives us.

Love in Him!

……

 

 

 

This email brought us a lot of tears, comfort, and praises to our Lord.

May God’s name be glorified.
