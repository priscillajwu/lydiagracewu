September 13, 2006, Wednesday

After Lydia lost all her hair, mom applied a hair wig from an organization called “Locks of Love” which would send the hair wig to the children cancer patients after screening.  Lydia was approved.  There were a lot of work and packages to send back and forth, in order to make sure the hair wig will nicely fit.  Lydia always patiently sat straight for a long time while mom worked on those models.  Lydia passed away while we were still waiting for the wig.  Of course, we called to cancel the order.  During those time, we learned that we could donate our hair (at least 10 inches) to them so they can give to the cancer patients who lost hair.

 

Priscilla shared with us last night about her conversation with Lydia.

While Lydia shared the hospital bed with Priscilla.  Lydia said,

“Your hair is getting long.”

“I want to keep until it’s at least 10 inches.”  Priscilla said.

“So you can donate to Locks of Love?” Lydia asked,

“Yes.”

“You do that for me?”

“Yeah”

“Oh, thank you, Priscilla.”

According Priscilla, Lydia thanked her quite a few times about this.

What a strong girl, while she looked at her sisters’ long hair and she lost all her hair.  Is it fair to her?  No.  She did not get upset, instead, she was still thankful.  Lydia also kept reminding herself,

“This is only temporary, my hair will grow back, although it may take several years to get to the length I had...”

She was always optimistic, and cheered herself up.

Mom recalled recently and concluded that Lydia’s strong faith and positive attitude that she will get well, actually helped mom greatly during those six and half months.

 

 

While I was miserably in sorrow earlier today, God gave me a sentence,

“Do not focus on the loss of Lydia, but think of the gain for God’s kingdom because of her ten years of life.”

 

Indeed, it helped.  Thank you for your prayers…
