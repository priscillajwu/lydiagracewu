September 2, 2006, Saturday

We finalized the design of the marker on the Lydia’s grave this morning.  It was not an easy thing to do emotionally.  The cost is no longer the concern.  Lydia deserves a nicely made marker.  Lydia’s 10 years of life will be remembered for years to come.

The marker has Lydia’s picture on it, and 2 Timothy 4:7.  An emblem of prayer hands and a cross will be clearly seen, just as Lydia did in her life.  The last thing she did on earth was pray to God, and the last words she said were “Jesus, help me”.

 

Our bible study cell group holds a gathering at one dear couple’s house.  There were some new friends.  We took turns to introduce the person sat next to us.  Most people were introduced with their spouse and how many children they have.  When it’s our turn, the number of our children was not mentioned.  Otherwise, the atmosphere will be saddened right away.  Really, I still not used to introduce myself that I have three daughters…  It sounds so weird.  For years, four girls have been the symbol of our family.  Like family in the novel “The Little Woman”.  Unfortunately, the third girl died young.

 

In the party, a two years old boy, who is very close to us, the host family’s youngest child, pointed to Priscilla and said to me, “She is your Priscilla.”

I was surprised he could talk a whole sentence now.  I asked him,

“Then, where is my Lydia?”

He was trying to look for her.  An older boy next to him said, “Lydia is there now.” with his index finger pointing upward.

I did not know why I asked a two years old boy this tough question.  It actually made me sad.  But I am thankful that children of God are for certain that our eternal life will be spent with the Lord.

 

Most people are seeking happiness in life.  It’s their purpose of life.  A friend forwarded us a short interview of Rick Warren (author of “Purpose Driven Life”).  Rick said the life is preparation for eternity.  Indeed, life could be full of hardship, pain, sadness, fear, and loss.  All of these could help us to build up godly characters, and fulfill the purpose of life which God gave to us.  Then, at the time we meet the Lord, we can hear from the Lord, “Well done, good and faithful servant…”(Matthew 25:21)

“One of the easiest ways to get rid of pain is to get your focus off yourself and onto God and others” said Rick Warren.  It’s truly a reminder to me.
