January 15, 2007, Monday

 

Birthday of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.

Two years ago, we visited Dr. Martin Luther King’s tomb and museum in Atlanta.  It was truly inspiring.

That day, I had a dream, that Lydia would, one day, like Dr. Martin Luther King, do the extraordinary work for the society.  I could almost picture that she become the Supreme Court Justice.  The same dream we had for her when we visited the Supreme Court in Washington DC couple years earlier.

We had no doubt that she should be a lawyer ever since she was as young as one year old.  Yes, she was already talking so much with decent logic at that age.

 

Or, she could be a great missionary, or a successful evangelist.  (She liked this better.)

 

But that is just a dream.  I am not sure it would ever come true.

 

I also had a dream for her, also for three other girls.  This is the most legitimate dream, almost not a dream.  Everybody can take it for granted.

 

It’s that she will grow up.

 

When this most ordinary dream was shattered, it’s extremely painful.  It’s like I am driving for a normal speed on the high way, then a big object hit me, my vehicle stopped and totally destroyed.  My head hit the windshield badly.  I am totally lost, not knowing what happened, and am so dizzy that I don’t know where to go on, and how…

 

 

It’s easy to say that we are faithful when things go as smoothly as we can expect.  The prayers were answered, and life generally goes in the right directions.

When the most ordinary dream was shattered, we were hurt to the bone, to the deepest in the heart, we could still say, “May the name of the Lord be praised.” (Job 1:21)

 

 

Nowadays, I realized that suffering is necessary.  The harder it gets, the more God can work on us.  We will love God more, and develop more passion for the Lord.

 

Otherwise, there would be full of spoiled brats in the heaven, then the heaven would not be attractive to me.

 

Even though I realize the suffering and trials can not be avoided and it’s how the life is, the true Christian’s life.

I still shiver simply by thinking about how difficult it is…
