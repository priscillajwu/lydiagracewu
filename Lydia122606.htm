December 26, 2006, Tuesday

Could not sleep again last night until 5 AM.

I recall when Lydia was ill, there were many sleepless nights we were on Lydia’s bed side.

Back then, we ached terribly and it was extremely painful to see Lydia suffering.  It was so difficult even just think about it of those days.

Now, again I have sleepless nights and my heart also aches and feels the extreme pain of the loss of Lydia, with the horrible emptiness.

If I had choice, I would rather go back to when Lydia was still alive.  I will give up everything for the exchange of Lydia still being around.

But it’s not my choice.  God has His sovereignty.  He decided and it will be done.

I have been thinking for the entire day.

There is no doubt that our God is faithful, loving, and caring.  I have experienced that for years.

But God is not just the Santa Claus.  He is the creator of the entire universe.  He can do all things, according to His will and ultimate wisdom.  And he will do it without consulting us.

While I am under the extreme sorrow and suffering and not yet clear about His plan for me (for Lydia, for my entire family, and maybe also for the people who know about Lydia’s story), all I can do is fear God, fear God, and fear God.

 

I see the purpose of life is not only to enjoy the life God has given us, but also suffer the agony God wants us to go through.

Christ Jesus is the best role model of suffering.

So that, we know the earth is not our home, but the eternity with our Lord.

Truly, the earthly life is so trivial.  It serves only one purpose, to prepare us for the eternal life.

Now, I truly long for the life without tears and sufferings.  In order to achieve that, I need to continue surviving and suffering.  And I believe this is the best way for me to get there, the way God has prepared for me.

It’s hard, deep into my soul, and I need to take the challenge and go on.
