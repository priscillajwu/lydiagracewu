December 2, 2006, Saturday

A few days ago, Mom took Esther to Lydia’s grave and decorated Lydia’s small grave and little tree behind it with a lot of Christmas decorations.  Esther said, “Even our home did not get decorated like that.”  Mom did not say anything when she heard it.

That same night, I took Deborah from school after music play practice.  Passing the neighborhood, we saw one house with beautiful Christmas lights.

“When are we going to get our Christmas light this year?”  Deborah asked me.

“We may not do it this year.”  I answered her.

“Oh…”  Deborah said a few seconds later, “But why not?”

“Because it’s just too difficult for me to do it.”  I told her.

 

For several days, the girls kept asking, “Can we have Christmas tree and light?”

My answer has been negative.

 

Today, for whatever reason, I decided to do it.  I cleaned up the yard and put lawn mower back in storage.  (It was so cold and freezing, I was not aware that my finger was cut, until it bled and dripped to other fingers and palm.)  Anyway, to make the girls happy, I continue working on the Christmas lights.  Deborah and Esther helped. (Good, they got Lydia’s spirit now.  Before, Lydia would jump up and down and announce to everybody that daddy is working on the Christmas lights.  I would work so hard seeing her so excited.)  Then, Christmas tree was up too.

Mom took out the ornament box.  The very first ornament on the top was a little kettle Lydia made during one of her hospital stay.  Lydia made sure mom put in the ornament box.

“Lydia made this in March,” Mom said, “After Lydia finished, she said ‘I will hang it on the Christmas tree myself this Christmas’...”

While saying it, mom could not hold it anymore.  She totally broke down, crying terribly.  It was so sad, very very sad.  We were all saddened to the bottom of our hearts and penetrated, pierced through.  In the moment like this, we want to wonder why is life so difficult.

 

We put two other ornaments Lydia made when she was younger together with this kettle ornament (CHMC, acronym of Children Hospital Medical Center).

Yes, it’s brave for us to put Christmas tree up, but it’s just too heartbreaking to look at the ornaments Lydia made. 

Can I stand it?  Oh, Lord, please give me strength.
