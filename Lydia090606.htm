September 6, 2006, Wednesday

A colleague reminded me that because we love Lydia so much, way too much, that is why every single memory of Lydia hurts greatly.

I had dentist appointment today.  My right side mouth got numbed (and caused right side lip numbed too).  After it, I tried to swish.  The water simply spilled out of my mouth by itself.  I had no control over it.  It was a strange feeling.  However, very quickly, I was back to normal.  It would be terrible if I were like that for a long period of time.

I thought about Lydia.  She was not able to control her lip for many days, all the way to the very end.  She was not able to talk clearly (it must be so tough for a girl who enjoy talking all her life), not able to hold water in her mouth, not able to swallow ...  Yet, she showed very little frustration or felt upset, or bad temper.  Instead, she kept saying sorry that she could not speak clearly, and she used her hands to help her lip close.  She believed she would get better.  That was only temporary.  She would get well again.  She truly believed in that.  So did mom and dad.  That was why when some doctors and nurses gave up hope very early on really made us very uncomfortable.

Another one.  Lydia had one dentist appointment after her first brain surgery.  She declined the option to get numbed.  She told dentist, this pain was nothing, compared with the pain she suffered from her brain tumor.

Esther’s high school curriculum review was held tonight.  Again, I was there alone without mom.  (Both of us went last year.)  Esther took two math subjects this year.  Lydia enjoyed math so much too.  I recalled before the first brain surgery, the neurosurgeon asked Lydia what grade was she in and what was her favorite subject.

“Math.” Answered Lydia right away without much thought about it.

“Wow, that is not usual!”

I guessed he never heard that answer from a little GIRL.

 

Mom washed Priscilla’s hair tonight (Mom did that pretty often for Lydia and Priscilla).  After it, Priscilla asked mom, “Do you know what Lydia would be doing now if she is still here?”, Priscilla continued, “She will be in the shower room with you.  You will be washing her hair, since mine was done.  It’s her turn.  She always let me go first.”  You knew mom’s reaction then, a lot of tears.

 

“I felt Lydia is here with me a lot.”  Priscilla said that to mom happily quite a few times lately.

Shouldn’t our thinking process be more like little child?  I would like to…

 

We know that we could not have any interaction with our loved one anymore until we meet again.  Although we hope we can now.  But it’s not possible.  Therefore, we need to hold on to the Lord tightly and ask for heavenly peace in our heart.  Otherwise, it is so difficult, and we can go crazy.  For this, I have to thank God for his continued blessing, grace, and comfort poured into my family.
