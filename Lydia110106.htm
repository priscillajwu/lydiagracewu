Nov. 1, 2006

 

A couple weeks ago, I bought some solar lights and put one at Lydia’s gravesite. I like it when the sky turns dark the light will light up automatically. But I never got to see the little solar light lights up, because it was never dark enough. Sunday evening, the first day without daylight saving time, I went there with Esther; we saw the little light lighting up. We joked that people might get scared of seeing the floating light in the totally dark cemetery. Quite interesting, we were not afraid of anything, on the other hand, I even wish I could see Lydia standing in front of me (even I know it is impossible!!).

 

When we discovered there were some fresh flowers in Lydia’s vase. My heart suddenly warmed up. I knew someone must have been visited here, for our beloved little Lydia who died at age at 10.

 

Everyday life continues to be really tough without Lydia being around. I try to live up my life to the Lord’s plan, is to glorify God’s name. I found my newly mission is to continue carrying on Lydia’s earthly mission to fulfill her dream of being a missionary. I learned that the wristbands we just made become a really good tool to reach both of the unbelievers and Christians, too. I really wish all the people who have been touched or inspired by Lydia’s story are willing to get one for themselves to wear and also get some to share to their friends. We will be so happy to hear from you to tell us how Lydia’s story continues inspiring and encouraging you. Because God is real! Even though He already took Lydia home, His work still continues.

 

We can’t do this alone. Would you be willing to be part of Lydia’s mission?
