August 10, 2006, Thursday

Today, Lydia starts to have difficulty to urinate. It would take 20 to 30 minutes just to urinate. She would pray, press her bladder, and struggle, simply trying to pee.

Once, after she finally got urine out, I told her, good job. She said, "What’s good job about it, I do this everyday."

To me, it’s huge defeat if that happens to me. But she took it lightly.

Also, today, her lips got even worse. She just could not close them; therefore, she could not speak clearly. She could not pronounce words with B, P, M, or V, F.

When she ate (she already has difficulty to put food into her mouth because her hand coordination and shaking), food would come out of her mouth. She could not control it. Watching her eating is one of the most difficult things in the world.

She found out a way. When drinking liquid or sucking through straw, or speak, she would use another hand to help closing her lips. It’s so sad and very difficult to look at her doing that. However, she said,

"It’s OK. I’ll try to improve it."

Her memory also got very bad. She tried to use walker today for the first time in her life. She worked very hard to walk to the vending machine and bought a bag of candy. It was a huge thing this afternoon. She forgot about it totally at night.

Around 5 PM today, all of sudden, she became talkative (that is Lydia, she must be feeling better), which have not happened for a while.

Quotes from Lydia today.

"Mom, why do you cry? I will get better."

"It’s all God’s will."

"Why you look so sad, mom, it’s OK..."

"Although mom and dad is with me all the time, but often, I am with myself during test, scan or MRIs. But I know God is with me."

"I know God loves me. God will help me."

“I need to get used to it.  I need to stay strong.”

Another one I could not share, it’s too emotional and too difficult to write it down.

I have been receiving some emails about miraculous healing. Thank God for He always gives me hope through brothers and sisters and friends.

Two of Lydia’s doctors said today in separate occasions,

"Lydia, you have never stopped amazing me."
