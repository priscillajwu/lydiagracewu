I had the pleasure of being Lydia's gifted teacher working with her in the classroom in first and second grade and then in the resource room as a third grader. However, my first memory of Lydia was at an Open House Night at Symmes Elementary when Lydia was probably 4 years old.

 

She and Priscilla were dressed in the sweetest little rag doll dresses.  I couldn't take my eyes off of them with their long black hair and beautiful faces.  Their dresses tied in a bow in the back and by this time of the night their bows were loose or untied altogether.  Pricilla was holding on to Lydia's bow as they were running circles around mom, giggling and being as silly as can be.  I remember thinking I have never seen anything so cute in all my life.  I thought right there how I couldn't wait to have a little girl.

 

If I were to describe Lydia I would say she was the perfect daughter.  I don't know if other teachers do this but over my 10 year career I have come across a couple students that I have thought "Oh if I could pick out my daughter or son that this is who I would pick."  I would look at their parents with envy and wonder if they knew how lucky they were to have such a perfect child.  I would prod them at conferences asking how did they do it.  Hoping they would let me in on the secret.  This was Lydia to me.

 

First of all on the surface, she couldn't have been more adorably cute.  You couldn't help but notice her.

 

And so sweet natured.  All the girls gravitated to Lydia at school.  I remember hearing her name constantly even when she wasn't in my classroom.  Lydia this, Lydia that.  The girls wanted to be just like her.

 

As her teacher, I admired how eager Lydia was to tackle any challenge.  Though learning came very easy to her, Lydia was so humble.  What I loved the most about Lydia though was that nothing would derail her determination.  If she didn't get the answer on the first try she persisted without hesitation and without feeling embarrassed.  This is rare among many gifted students who are often devastated when they are told to try again.

 

A sweet little angel on earth is how I would describe Lydia.  Lovely in every sense of the word.
