October 20, 2006, Friday

A missionary from Thailand mountain villages came to share tonight.  I was always amazed about his stories in the mission field.  He serves in the very poor villagers, who have very little in materials.

He came to visit us last year and I introduced my four daughters to the missionary.  Maybe one day, I thought, one of the girls would have mission trip or work there.

“One of my daughters passed away just two months ago.”  I told him tonight, “Now I have three daughters.”

“I heard about it. I am very sorry.”  He said sincerely, “Lydia’s life was in the Lord’s hand, yours and mine also.”

“Yes.”  I agreed.  I know he (only in his 40s) has very serious heart attack not long ago.

“I had heart attack, but now I am serving almost as hard as before.”  He smiled at me.

Yes, he is on the road all the time, spread gospel from a village to another village, saving souls, and casting out demons.

I saw a spiritual role model in front of me.  Very humble, capable of speaking six to seven languages.  He could be a good businessman and make a good fortune.  However, he saw God’s kingdom more important and to put his life in the mission field.

For whatever reason, he brought me comfort without saying much to comfort me.  We do not know each other very well, but I know he suffers greatly for the Lord.  He always showed joy and peace on his face.  Amazing, I think, because God is amazing.

 

Mom brought Lydia’s wristband to the church tonight.  Many brothers and sisters, and children liked it a lot.  More than hundred took and donated.  We will be able to use the money to support this missionary’s work.

 

Late in the night, Esther was frustrated and talked with me.  She liked a certain musical skill, but was not willing to put enough effort into it.

“Why couldn’t you do it?”  I was very upset, “Lydia would be able to do it.  I know Lydia is not perfect, but she did her best into everything she did.”

“Yes, but I am not Lydia, I am just not as good as she is.”  Esther cried.

“What do you mean?  If your little sister, five and half years younger than you, could do it.  I don’t get why can’t you do it.”  I shed some tears too.

Finally, we agreed that we need to pray more about whatever things we do.

After Esther went to sleep, I wondered myself why was I so mean to her, and I did poorly as a father although terribly grieving.  While suffering, I guess, we need more of God’s grace.

 

Is it because Lydia has learned the difficult lessons that normally take people decades or entire life to learn?  So God took her, “Well done, good and faithful servant! …… Come and share your master’s happiness!”  (Matthew 25:21)

 

Many years ago, I saw a quote, “God put me in this world to accomplish certain things.  So far, I am terribly behind.  So, I will never die.”  Very sarcastic.  Really, if I am like that, I need to be very afraid.
