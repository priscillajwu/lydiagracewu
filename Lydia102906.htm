October 29, 2006, Sunday

This is a typical Sunday.

We went to the church, led the worship, taught the Sunday school, attended two church meetings in the afternoon.  Came home in the evening and fixed meals, checked kids’ homework and got ready for school tomorrow.  It seemed to be a busy day.  However, my heart continues very heavy.  Sorrow attacked many times today.

Leaves fell all over the front yard and backyard.  It’s especially difficult for me.  I was not able to pick up the rake to rake the leaves.  That would make me extremely sad.  Recalled many times in the fall, Lydia enjoyed so much raking the leaves.  We had so much fun raking the leaves and put into bags.  I am just not able to rake the leaves without Lydia yet.

Life is so different now.  I could not believe raking leaves would become such a challenge for me.

Mom’s eyes got infected pretty bad.  She went to see the doctor and got two antibiotics; one to apply in the eye, and another taken by mouth.  She could not use the contacts for two weeks, her heavy glasses made her very uncomfortable.  The cause of her eye infection, I think, is crying too much.  Very simple.

My heartache continues, my stomachache continues.  Although life goes on, it’s so different now.  People may see us the same as before, but I see the world very different now.

People often asked us, “I can not imagine how you can handle this.”

I have no answer for them.  I have the same question to myself.  I can only pray very very hard.  That is the best way I can do or think. 

I can also feel why Naomi said the sentences in the book of Ruth 1: 20-21.
