November 24, 2006, Friday

This is the biggest shopping day of the year, the day after Thanksgiving.  Mom and Esther got up early morning, only to find out that the electronic stores and apparel stores were packed with too many people.  The Christian bookstore was less crowded though.

At Christian bookstore, mom bought a Veggie Tale (Christian cartoon characters) game for Priscilla.  It was the idea of Lydia.  Lydia wanted to buy this game for Priscilla using the gift card Lydia had.  However, it was sold out when Lydia visited the Christian bookstore.  Now, although Lydia already passed away, mom still got it for Priscilla.

“Priscilla, this is the gift for you…from Lydia.” Mom said to Priscilla, almost choked up.

Priscilla could not say a word when she received it.

 

Mom also bought some Christmas music CDs.  She put in the CD player.  Oddly, when Christmas music was played, only made me very sad.  So sad that I felt sorrow attacked me constantly as long as the music was playing.  I am surprised to realize that I could not stand to hear Christmas music.

 

The weather was warmer than usual late November, I suggested going to the nearby park to hike.  Nobody would like to.  I forgot again.  Only Lydia would like the idea of hiking.  Dummy daddy, Lydia is not around anymore.

 

At least, we were able to play tennis outdoors.  Only Deborah and Priscilla went.  Esther would rather stay home to do homework.  (Before, Lydia would urge Esther to go as well.)  While playing, I could recall that Lydia always played most diligently, and would be willing to pick up the scattered tennis balls for the sisters to play.

 

Some said God only takes the best.  That seems to be very true.
