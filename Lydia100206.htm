October 2, 2006, Tuesday

An excerpt of the email we received over the weekend from Esther’s teacher,

“I, as many others, have been following Lydia's story and your family's journey through this challenging time ever since March or April when Esther gave me the website address.  I was Esther's teacher last year.  Esther is a very special student...very gifted, conscientious, kind-hearted, helpful, respectful and caring of others' feelings.  I never met Lydia but from what I have learned she had many similar qualities.  I am sure that Deborah and Priscilla are lovely girls, as well.  Thank you so much for sharing your story.  I know that it has GREATLY touched me and changed my life in so many ways.  On a daily basis I pray for your family and appreciate my own family a bit more.  I try not to get upset about the little challenges that face each of us every day.  After all, if Lydia can be so positive when faced with such pain and hardship, can't I wait patiently in a traffic jam?  If Lydia can think of herself BEFORE others, can't I take time on a busy night to make a meal for a sick friend?  These are just examples but I often think of Lydia when faced with a major OR minor challenge lately. She has also caused me to think deeply about my own faith in God.  Her ability to see beyond this life...to realize that her PURPOSE on earth is to prepare herself for eternity with God is astounding, amazing and awe-inspiring.  Your family has given me a huge gift.  I have an 18-month-old little boy.  Of course, we go to church, we pray together, we read Bible stories and sing hymns of praise with him even though is still so young.  I am so inspired by the all-encompassing FAITH of your four daughters.  Deborah, Priscilla and Esther possess the same ability to remember what is important in this life.  They understand that Lydia is where we all aspire to be one day.  We are struggling with our day to day lives and she is in eternal paradise with our Heavenly Father.  This is what I need to teach my son.  NOTHING else matters in this life but to live for God with the end goal of spending eternity in His presence.  God loves the Wu family and trusted you with Lydia.  You are very blessed to have been able to nurture her little soul and send her back to her Father.  He chose YOU for this awesome task and you passed the test.

……”

Yes, dear sister in the Lord.  To believe in God is not enough, we need to LIVE for God.  Thank you so much for sharing.  We were very touched and inspired.

 

In the evening, I got a wound on my finger.  Before, all I needed to do is call for the little nurse.  Lydia has been the little nurse in the family since she was very little.  Whenever anybody in the family got paper cut or little wound.  Right away, Lydia would put down whatever she was doing and rushed to get bandage with ointment and come to take care of us.  She was always ready to take care of others.  Before, I never truly appreciated that.  I just let her do it. I thought she enjoyed doing it, and I enjoyed letting her do it.  Tonight, while I put bandage on my own finger, my eyes were full of tears.  All these memories, although sweet, hurt so much.

 

Before bedtime, there was some radio and beeping noise from Priscilla’s bedroom.  Mom heard and went to find out what’s going on.

“I want to get up by myself.”  Priscilla said, “I am trying to set the alarm clock.”

Priscilla was working on the alarm clock.  She spent quite some time, but still not able to get it.  For years, Lydia set the alarm clock in their bedroom.  Lydia would get up first and then wake up Priscilla.  If Lydia left home earlier for double accelerated math class, then mom would wake up Priscilla in the morning for school.  So, Priscilla never learned how to use alarm clock.  While showing Priscilla how to set alarm clock, again, my heart just ached…

 

Life nowadays is just much harder than when Lydia was ill.  God gave us a lot of faith back then and a lot of energy.  Now, He has given us the true hope.  However, the separation with Lydia is just so difficult.  It’s so hard that whenever I am alone, I can cry so bad until my eyes hurt and stomach ache.  Some shared with us that we have so much tears because we love her so much.  We should be joyful while we cry, because we love, and that is the gift from the Lord…

 

Sorrow and suffering, nobody wants it.  But is it preventable?  I can probably deny it or ignore it and go on.  That is probably easy way out.  (And a lot of people do that subconsciously, if they do not have God.)

Now, I can appreciate the salvation a lot more when Son of God sacrificed His life for our sins.  It was so precious.  It was ultimately heart breaking for the Lord and our heavenly father.
