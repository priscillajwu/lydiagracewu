September 12, 2006, Tuesday

Ever since Lydia passed away, we have been having a lot of rainy days in Cincinnati in last three weeks.  It definitely did not help relieving our grief.  Lydia did not like rainy days.  But she knew that the rain is very important to us.  When she was little, she said to mom, “I hope it would rain during the night when people sleep, and rain would stop before people get up.”  Recently, it rained day and night, just like our tears…

 

We were eating the type of noodle Lydia liked a lot.  Mom broke into tears while thinking that Lydia could not enjoy the noodle again.

“Mom, Lydia is eating it right now in heaven, and probably noodle banquet.”  Deborah said.  Mom heard and smiled.

 

I went to the building committee meeting which I have been missing for months when Lydia was ill.  Recall Lydia asked me many times,

“Daddy, when will our church be built?”  Back then, I was a bit annoyed after she asked so many times.

“Just pray and God will provide to us in His timing.”

“OK.”  Lydia said.

Another sad feeling that Lydia would never get chance to see the new church building when it’s finished.  However, she probably won’t mind now since the buildings in heaven have to be millions times better than the earthly buildings.

In a way, she should be also very happy that people donated to the building fund in memory of her.  The amount of money, she would never be able to raise, if she were still alive.

It amazed me that she would often thought about church building project, while most kids probably don’t care or thought it’s adult’s business.

 

I do not understand why God took Lydia away from us.  I will know that later when I meet the Lord myself.

Right now I want to carry out many things inspired by Lydia or her wishes because her love of the Lord Jesus…
