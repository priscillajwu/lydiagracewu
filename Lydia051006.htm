May 10, 2006, Wednesday

Five years ago, Esther won the school spelling bee competition when she was in fourth grade. Two years ago, Deborah won the same spelling bee competition when she was a fourth grader.

Now, as a fourth grader, Lydia did not want to be part of it at all. We knew it’s a lot pressure on her and she has been through a lot lately. But Deborah strongly encouraged Lydia at least to try. Even that gave Lydia a lot of stress and she cried badly. She just did not want to do it. I agreed that she does not need to compete if she does not want to. To me, as long as she could survive from the cancer and the harsh treatments, to be a good speller or not, is the least of my concerns. However, with encouragement from her teacher and classmates, she participated in the class qualifier run, and she was one of the four qualifying students chosen to represent her class.

To my surprise, and also everybody else’s surprise, she not only participated, but also won the school spelling bee competition today. I can only give the glory to our God, because God has mercy and grace on her.

We were told that the radiation treatment to the brain might have permanent damage to the patients, which could show up later. She may have learning difficulties and many other possible problems for years to come. We can only pray that God’s grace and mercy will continue to be with her…

We asked her why she won, while she really had a very slim chance to win (being so ill and out of school for several months.) What lesson does God want us to learn?

"For the sick, do not lose hope." Lydia said.

 

Tomorrow, she will have another blood test. Please pray for her blood count and appetite.
