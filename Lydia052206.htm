May 22, 2006, Monday

My dear prayer heroes,

I know many of you have been praying for us, especially for today’s MRI.

Knowing many are praying for us, brought us comfort, and carried us through the day.

We had a very very rough day.

We were told to arrive in the Cincinnati Children Hospital by 9:30 AM. So, Mom and Lydia did. Upon arrival, they were told there will be some delay. So, they waited, and waited... Lydia was hungry and weak, and not feeling well. Her discomforts (mainly headache) grew as the waiting seems to be forever.

Minutes past, hours past, nobody came to explain what’s going on. None at all. Mom tried to ask how much longer it will be, the attitude from the hospital was really bad.

By the time Lydia got her turn of MRI was in the afternoon. They arrived home in the late afternoon.

Lydia was not in good shape at all after arriving home. She got fever again, and both her eyes were very red. Her headache was so bad, almost like during her recovery from surgeries. (It’s hard to believe she was doing quite well over the weekend. Is it too much exposure and activities again like last week?)

We were very scared and worried. I had to keep praying to calm down. Several times, we were going to call the hospital, but did not. It was such a struggle. We knew, once we called, we need to go to emergency room, and will be more waiting and suffering. Be frankly, we’d rather stay home.

We kept praying, Lydia’s fever came down a little bit. Other girls need some helps and attentions from us as well during the night. They dared not to ask for. But they did need help, it was Monday night. It was a highly stressful night.

So, Mom took care of other girls. I tried to get Lydia to sleep.

Lydia was so in discomfort, she banged her head, rubbed her head against the pillow. She had itchiness all over her body, I have to apply different kinds of lotion to her bald head, and her back (The sun burn from radiation still troubled her.) I kept massaging her head and right leg (We believe there was nerve damage to her right leg during the first brain surgery, and she has been suffering from it ever since.) She was not feeling well at all, she has to keep scrolling around her bed. I was so busy applying lotion, massaging, and calming her down. Look at my little daughter in so much pain, my heart was aching, and I cried badly inside.

"What is going on?" I cried out in my heart.

Both Mom and I developed pretty bad stomachaches tonight.

It is now way past midnight. I still got anger toward the radiology department in the Children’s Hospital.  But, I know I shouldn’t. I should have forgiven them by now.  Maybe, they were in trouble themselves, that was probably why things got so messed up today for them.

I am praying very hard. I have no reason not to believe God has continued healing Lydia. Her headache may be from the fever, cold, viral infection, or simply weakness, and nothing to do with the tumors.

Please do not lose hope, and please continue praying for Lydia and us. We pray that, if it’s God’s will, and our God is merciful and graceful, the report of this MRI will be encouraging.

And, since many are praying for us, the Lord must have allowed whatever happened today. I just need to be obedient to the Lord, and learn the lessons from it.

I know our God has great mercy and grace. Truly, the Lord is my only hope. Nothing else I can depend on.

In Him,

Simon
