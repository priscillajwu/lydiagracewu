November 10, 2006, Friday

It’s a particular warm today in the season of fall.

I felt relieved that I was able to get rid of a lot of tree leaves in the yard using the lawn mower, without using the rakes.  For weeks, I was afraid to use rake to clean up the leaves, which caused me a lot of heartache because of thinking of Lydia.  (For Lydia raked the leaves every fall and enjoyed a lot.)

God has used various ways to comfort us.  I was able to talk to quite a few brothers and sisters at church about Lydia (before, during, and after the bible study).  That helped a lot.  Often, people around us seemed reluctant to talk about Lydia.  And that hurts us.  But I do need to have sympathy.  Maybe people do not know how to talk about Lydia, or not feel comfortable to talk about Lydia, or they don’t know how to handle it themselves.

Now that we have experience, we may be able to help others later.  Nobody wants this kind of experience, but do we have choice?

 

 

“We were able to get acquainted with a lot of friends, brothers and sisters in the Lord recently.  That is very nice.”  Mom said.

“But with too high a price to pay.” I replied.

“Lydia may have to go no matter what.  But all these friends were the blessing from the Lord.”  Mom said.

I agreed, with a lot of grief in my heart though.
