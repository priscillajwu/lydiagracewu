November 15, 2006, Wednesday

We received this email two days ago, from the very good friend and classmate of Lydia…

“

I will never forget

 

When my best friend Lydia Wu died.  It was the most depressing day of my entire life!  I was exhausted from not getting enough sleep because I couldn’t get Lydia out of my mind.  I was so full of sorrow, my heart felt like it was breaking. I slumped to her funeral a few days later and saw her stiff, silent body just lying there.  It didn’t look like her, but I knew it was from that caring look on her face that she always had. She was the most caring person I’ve ever met who never let anyone get upset, and always had a smile on her face!

 

While we were sitting in the pews waiting for the service to begin, I saw mine and Lydia’s teacher, Mrs. Jones.  I also saw our principal and a lot of my friends who were also crying.  “Can we leave early?” I asked my mom.  I didn’t want to stay there because I knew it would make me even more depressed. We decided to stay though, and I’m glad we did because I know it made Lydia’s family feel happy that her friends were there.

 

 After the funeral, I got a chance to talk to my friends about Lydia and they were all as depressed as I was. We didn’t think that it was fair for her to have died so young!  We also felt horrible for her family. I can’t imagine how sad it would be if someone in my family died! Lydia was one of my best friends, and I’ll never forget her.  Some day, I know we’ll be together again.  You were my best friend Lydia, and I’ll never forget you!!

“

We shed a lot of tears while reading this paper.  The friendship, the sorrow, the memory, and the hope, are so touching.  Yes, some day we will all be together again.  Praise the Lord.

 

 

Mom had to see the eye doctor again today.  It’s the second time.  Her both eyes still got infection.  The cause is again crying too much.  These two trips to the eye doctor were under heavy pouring rains (strange that it was raining only during these two trips, not before or after).  Mom felt that the Lord has been grieving with us.  That was comforting.  Isn’t that amazing how God helped us from many ways?
