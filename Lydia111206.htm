November 12, 2006, Sunday

This morning, entire family, five of us (of course, not six of us), went to church in a small car.  That was extremely strange experience.  I do not recall any occasion that this small car would accommodate the entire family.  This car was bought the year Priscilla was born.  I drove it to work everyday, not intended for the entire family.  Even after Lydia passed away, we still used to use big mini-van for the entire family.

Today, since Esther would like to drive to church, we let her drive the small car.  We fit into the car just fine.  The ride to the church was OK.  However, it was quiet, quieter than when Lydia was among us.  Back then, always a lot of laughter and screaming.  Sometimes annoyed, I wanted Lydia to be quiet, now we dearly missed the old good time, and her quick wit for many things.

 

After church, I suggested we go to eat noodle.  While waiting for the food, I asked three girls,

“Are you used to that Lydia was around us yet?”

“It feels weird.”  Deborah answered.

Well, Thanksgiving and Christmas are around the corner, I can foresee more tough time ahead of us.  Hey, no fear, we have faith God will help us, just like He has been helping us for last eighty three days after Lydia passed away, or during seven months when Lydia was ill, or our lives so far.

 

Over this weekend, several friends mentioned to us (in person or email) that Lydia would not like to see us grieving that much, and even hurt our health.

Thank you for caring for us.  Tell the truth, we are all trying very hard to live a normal life and to cheer up.  We did our best.  And we know we need to trust the Lord even more everyday.  Sorrow attacks us many times a day.  To stand still took a lot of strength and effort.  It’s easier just to imagine (like many people told us).  It’s very difficult to live it or to describe it, or to put in words.

Trust the Lord is essential, and we thank God for being able to do that.
