December 7, 2008, Sunday

 

Holidays are difficult for us.  Now, Thanksgivings has come and go (we hosted many families at my house like good old days), and Christmas is coming.  It’s still pretty dreadful.  But that is OK, for God is watching us and He provides comfort.

 

After six years of serving in the church elders and deacons’ board, I attended my last meeting as a deacon today.  Six years is the maximum years one can serve according to our church by-law.  Anyone can serve between two to six years.  Many probably can testify that it was not an easy job.

I have been serving in the board for several years with joyful heart from the Lord.  But after Lydia got sick, it became an extra burden.

Recall during Lydia’s illness, whenever there was a deacons board meeting.  I would struggle.  I was not sure either I should stay home to take care of her, or simply stayed home to be with her. 

In fact, I really did not want to go.  I wanted to be with her.  Besides, I really did not have much strength to take care more stuff beside endless hospital visits, home needs, work demands, routine church services, and other kids transportation needs.  It was simply too much.

But Lydia would say, “No, Daddy, I think you should go.  To serve God.  I will be OK.” 

Wow!

Of course, that was how we taught her and raised her.  But while I was weak and wanted to quit or take easy, she would not.  So, I went just about every single deacon meeting back in 2006 and afterwards. 

Well, now I have fulfilled my commitment to the Lord.  No less, and no more.  I stick to the end.

 

However, recall the conversation with Lydia about attending the meeting or not, it still brought quite a bit of sorrow and pain.

 

Lydia has lived a faithful life.  As her father, I just fulfilled another job God has given me.  For me, more to come.  For her, she has finished.  Although I am behind her, but think that she has done a great job of her life,

Then there is comfort.
