August 15, 2006, Tuesday

In the morning, Lydia had some neurological symptoms. Nurses quickly notified the doctor. After examining Lydia, the doctor told us the end for Lydia is anytime now.

Awfully saddened and with huge doubt to whatever doctor said, we got Esther, Deborah, (Priscilla is already here), and grandparents rushed to the hospital.

"I love you mommy", "I love you daddy", "I love you Esther", "I love you Deborah", "I love you Priscilla", Lydia kept saying "I love you" to everyone in the family, again and again, and non-stopping for hours.

"I love you too, Lydia." Everyone was crying badly. Worst ever.

Pastors, friends, started to show up to see Lydia. Lydia kept saying "I love you" to everyone who came to see her.

My mind and heart were broken into pieces. I was torn apart. Surviving is the most difficult thing for me at those moments.

We all kept praying and crying out to God for His healing. We could not believe this is it. There are so many people praying for Lydia…

A few hours later, Lydia became more stable and fell asleep, people started to leave. However, the doctor warned the death could be hours or couple days from now.

Scared by the doctor, although still believe God is going to heal Lydia, all of us in the family stayed in the hospital with Lydia tonight.

Lydia had tremendous sufferings and discomforts throughout the rest of the day. All of us tended to her at the same time.

It was too much suffering for a little ten year old girl to bear. We were in the extreme sorrow. Our hearts broken into pieces while we watching Lydia.

Also, the entire day, again and again (hundreds of time), Lydia prayed

"Lord Jesus, please help me to get well."

It’s extremely difficult for us to hear her saying that. She still continues having great faith and hope.

At night, in the family bedtime prayers, we cried out to God for His healing. It’s too cruel for us to lose Lydia. Loving heavenly father, please have mercy on us…
