October 4, 2006, Wednesday

Very slowly, mom continue cleaning up Lydia’s stuff.  Mom found Lydia’s devotion book today.

“Lydia insisted to have devotion every day.”  Holding the book, Mom said to me with tearful eyes, “We brought this devotion book to the hospital every single time.  If she did not do it someday, the next day, she would make it up consistently.  Only until this last hospitalization she could not do it since she could no longer see clearly.”

At the end of Lydia’s life, she knew, (and we all should know too), that, nothing, nothing is more important than to be with the Lord, to read bible, and to pray to our heavenly father.  We don’t have to wait until we are dying to know what is important in life.

 

A neighbor, in her 60s, who lost her husband last year shared with me today that she is currently attending a support group of widows and widowers.  One widow in the group recently lost her husband and her child.

“That must be so difficult for her.” I said.

“According to her, losing her husband was very difficult, but losing a child, was a lot worse.”  The neighbor said. “I just could not imagine what you are going through...”

 

Well, many people said that to me, “I can only imagine…”

Every time when I read that or heard that, I would mumble in my mind, “it’s a lot worse than you can imagine.”

I lost my father several years ago.  He has lived a full life.  One of my brothers died many years ago in an accident involved him and I when we were both teenagers.  Both times were very difficult.  But, this time, losing Lydia, was totally different.  “A lot worse” is not worse enough to describe it.

Only God, God knows why.  And for sure I would like to ask Him why He took Lydia away.  But now, knowing that I will not get answer in the current life, I believe, God knows better.  And I will agree with Him when I know the answer in the future.

I continue asking for God’s comfort and help when I was alone, when I could not sleep, when I wept terribly…

For God is good… really good…, otherwise I would be in “a lot worse” shape…

Praise the Lord.
