February 6, 2007, Tuesday

We received this email today, from Lydia’s friend’s mom,

“Last night, my daughter came to me. She held up a red t-shirt and said to all of us, "Tomorrow is Lydia's half birthday and the day she was diagnosed. I am wearing red tomorrow. You all should."

It is a cold day today, actually it is freezing. Wearing red brings some warmth. Just like Lydia does. She brings us closer to our creator, the undying love and source of warmth.”

 

Indeed, we got a winter storm today.  It snowed the entire afternoon…

 

One year has passed since that dreadful day.

I have very mixed feeling today.  Very hard to describe.  Very complex.  Very hard to think clear.

I decided to spend hours in the evening to clean the driveway.  I used a small snow shovel, so it took a lot longer to finish the work.

I kept thinking about Lydia.  A life so dear to me, now no longer around.  I could not get used to it.  Her absence in my life is so awkward.  It brought me a lot of heartaches.  My heart broke again and again.  Nobody could be with me 24 hours to comfort me.  Actually, I felt that most of the time, I am alone.  I have to keep thinking of God to keep me sane.

 

Many people tried very hard to avoid or prevent the adversity in life.  When it happened, they then tried to ignore, or deny it with addiction in earthly things or habits.  That is so unwise.  No gain to the faith, soul and spirit.  I refuse to do that.

 

February 6, 2006, the day my worse nightmare started.

And it was also the day that God’s name can be glorified in His way, in a very special way, through, SUFFERING of Lydia and her godly characters which revealed in this agony…

 

Christ Jesus is our Lord, our roll model.  With total trust in Him, I will go on…  with my soul, and my spirit.

 

May His peace, joy and grace be with you all…
