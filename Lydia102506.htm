October 25, 2006, Wednesday

One of Lydia’s fourth grade classmate’s father shared with me today that his son had hard time to deal with Lydia’s death.  Indeed, it must be very difficult to cope or to understand for many children who knew about Lydia.  We pray that the impact can lead to the positive memory, and everybody who knew Lydia or heard about Lydia can appreciate life, and live a godly life, since life can be very short.

One sister in church email me that last Friday she got two Lydia bracelets.  She gave one to her son, and another one to a girl of her neighbor who came to church with her.  The girl’s parents were nonbelievers.  With bracelets, they had discussion about where Lydia is now; yes, in heaven with the Lord.  At the end, this sister was able to help the girl believe in God and teach her how to pray.  It was very encouraging when I read that.  Lydia’s life and her story can really be used to save many souls, young and old…

Another sister shared with us that her husband, a non-believer, attended Lydia’s funeral service.  He was deeply touched.  He started to consider the Christian faith.  For non-believer, death is horrible, no comfort, no answer, and no hope whatsoever.

I know many are thinking and still waiting.  Many thought they would believe in God when they get older, attend church when they have time.  They can not devote their life to the Lord now, they have too many other priorities in life to do.

Don’t we understand that without God, life is meaningless, whatever we can do or accomplish are also meaningless.  When we died, earthly things became useless.  Only the faith of the Lord counts.

One more day delay to follow the Lord, one more day in life was wasted.

Think about Lydia, and we should remember ourselves, that life is short, do not waste in things without eternal value.
