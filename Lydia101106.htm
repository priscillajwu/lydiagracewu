October 11, 2006, Wednesday

I don’t know if there is something called sorrow attack in medical terms.  That is how I felt more than several times a day.  A thought of Lydia, or something related would trigger the sorrow attack.  Sometimes, it just happened for no reason.  I would feel hard to breath, impulse to cry, and might also come with the abdominal pain, or piercing heart pain.  I told mom about it.  She also had very similar experience.

I guess this type of feeling is normal for people who grieve.  Many people told us time would heal.  Some said for the loss of the child, it would take forever to heal.  Well, maybe time may help, but there will be new grief.  The grief and suffering will come, just matter of when.  (Ecclesiates 3:4).

I have to find peace and joy in the Lord.  I pray a lot, and read a lot of Scriptures.  And because of my deep grief, the peace and joy are a lot more precious and significant.

 

We received a few emails today, all from Esther’s teenage friends. 

One of emails shared,

“……Lydia passed away on my 16th birthday. August 21st, 2006. I will forever remember Lydia when my birthday comes around. I think that it is not only a celebration for me but one for her redemption day to be with the Lord and to end her suffering……

I have learned not to take any one day in life for granted through Lydia and her story. Her story continues to touch me in my everyday life. This week something rough happened to me but through the life of Lydia, I learned it wasn’t so bad and to keep on pushing through life and it will get better……”

 

Another one shared,

“Although I did not know Lydia personally, and I had only met her at church events when she was with her sisters, she has significantly changed my life.

A friend that I have been trying to reach out to for years recently showed an openness to God that I hadn't yet seen. Before, he had rejected every notion of God, dismissing Christianity as unreasonable. Yet he suddenly opened up this time, showing hope in knowing God. As I was in shock as to what had changed his mind, he mentioned that he had heard Lydia's story, and it had completely amazed him. How could a person, not to mention a ten year-old little girl, have such faith in something so intangible?

Although I was close to giving up on spreading the Gospel to this person, this miracle from Lydia reminded me not to quit. I remembered reading all the e-mails you have posted from people who have also been reminded not to give up. Now I have my own story. Lydia's faith had paved a path in my friend's heart that I could now take, and I felt so inspired by this little girl. Tears welled up in my eyes as I considered this blessing that Lydia had unknowingly bestowed upon me.

 

As I think about how much you and your family are suffering, I know I do not know how much it hurts. Yet Lydia's life and death have not been in vain, and she will continue to touch the hearts of many. I thank you for raising such an amazing child in God, and although I am only seventeen, I hope to one day be able to say that my children are as godly as your Lydia was. Thank you again for blessing all of us with her story, and I hope that my own story gives you some joy……”

 

Yes, it’s a great joy and praise the Lord.

There were a lot of people wrote to us in late August, and September.  There were so many.  Sorry that I started to share the emails we received only until recently.  And that was inspired by a couple emails asking for the possibility to be able to read the sharing and emails we received.

Although the emails I posted, I could not show from whom or where, but we want to sincerely thank many of you who sent us emails for your love, care, and prayers for us.

We also received lots lots of cards.  You have touched our hearts so much.  Reading your cards brought us a lot of comforts.

 

May God bless you and may God’s name be glorified.
