August 1, 2006, Tuesday

"How were you today." I asked Lydia, first thing I came home from work.

"Terrible." Lydia said without even looking at me.

"What kind of terrible?"

"Very."

"Give me some description." I asked.

"Very."

"Where do you not feeling well?"

"Head hurts, stomach hurts, I threw up…." Lydia said without much patience.

She has become very stubborn and irritable recently. We tried a little bit of discipline. But it could cause hours or days of bad consequence (cause her not feeling well). I think her endurance of stress is very very low. We really could not tell if it’s the post-surgical trauma, the side effects of radiation or chemo, or the side effects of many medicines (like steroid) she is taking now. Or she just did it on purpose because of great discomfort she is suffering. We could only pray for the patience and the wisdom from our Lord.

To cheer her up, we agreed that this week, right before her next chemotherapy, when her blood counts had gone up. We would bring her to the movie she wanted to see, the pirate movie. So, tonight mom took the girls to watch the movie. At least, we were glad that she came home in a better mood.

Earlier today, a group of aunts from a nearby church, who are also prayer warriors for Lydia, came to visit Lydia. That made Lydia happy too. We thank the Lord for the love and care from the body of the Christ, for He provides comforts through many ways.

Recall that Lydia was diagnosed with malignant brain tumors when she was exactly 9 and half years old.  Back then, quite a few times, she expressed her fear for not able to live to see herself turn ten year old.  Praise the Lord, the month of August is finally here.  She will be 10 years old in a few days.  I joked with Esther and Deborah when they turned ten.  I said they became as old as I am, the two digits number of years of age.  We are all exciting.  Our God is amazing, for He has full of mercy and grace.
