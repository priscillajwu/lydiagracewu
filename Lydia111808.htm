To Uncle Simon and family:

Hope you are doing well. I apologize for my ineloquence and the naivety of my thoughts, but I felt compelled to write this - I hope it helps in some way.

I thought of Lydia today, and decided to check Uncle Simon's blog for updates. It pains me to see that your grieving continues, and it breaks my heart when I read the sorrow in your writing. Yet at the same time, the immense and impassioned love with which you loved your daughter touches me and continues to touch me daily. I was blessed to have known Lydia, to have talked to her, taught her, joked with her, watched her smile, heard her laugh, heard her sing. Every time I think of her and her amazing faith, I am simultaneously humbled and inspired, and reminded of God's blessing and power. There is no question that she profoundly affected my life.

Since leaving Cincinnati, I've met some amazing people, many with extraordinary accomplishment and promise - people you meet, and think "they'll become somebody someday." But when I am with these new people and conversation turns to the amazing people we have known in our lives, the first person that I speak of is Lydia. And there is little doubt in my mind that many years from now, when my bones are old and brittle, that, more than the presidential-wannabes, more than the corporate legends, after ambitions, careers, and the material of this world fade to grey, I will continue speak of Lydia. And that years from now, when I think of Lydia, she will remain as always: a woman of God who loved and knew how to love, who inspired, who had immaculate faith as only a child can have, who epitomized strength.

I cannot claim to empathize, to know the unimaginable pain of losing someone so dear to your heart. I am too naive, too young in the world to do so. But I do know that, like thousands of others, Lydia touched my life because of her love, and the love of Christ that was shown through her. And similarly, that your incredible love for your daughter, the love that drove you to raise her, to pray for her, to sit by her side, to hold her hand, and that now causes you to grieve and suffer - that this love and its manifestation is in no way punishment from God or an indication of failure, but instead something genuinely amazing to be celebrated, something to be cherished, and something that inspired and continues to inspire those around you. As a son, witnessing such abounding love touches me in the deepest of senses, and I hope that one day I might be able to love my child as you loved yours. We were all blessed to have known Lydia, and even more, permit my youth to say this, you were blessed to have raised, to have known, and to have loved so dearly and intensely Lydia for her ten beautiful years on this earth. I pray that in your darkest days along this journey, that you might remember this, and that the Lord may be with you and give you strength.

As always, you will be in my prayers.

Love,
