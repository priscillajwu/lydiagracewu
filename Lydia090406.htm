September 4, 2006, Monday

It’s the Labor day holiday today, and we stayed home for the entire day.  It’s been two weeks since Lydia passed away. It felt like only yesterday.  We tried a little bit to clean up Lydia’s stuff.  It was so difficult.  Our hearts ached and felt so sad.  It’s like the heart is pressing the stomach.  Both heart and stomach felt terribly.  We had to stop and just talked about Lydia…

One thing we felt so frustrated is that Lydia used to be the person who facilitated the communication in the family.  She could speak and express so well.  When we tried to say something, after half a sentence, she already got what we wanted to say, and she would finish the sentence for us.  So, some of us became so lazy in talking.  Lydia simply talked everything.  She talked so much.  She probably talked more than everybody else in the family combined.  Quite a few times, I wanted her to shut up, and blamed her that some of us had lost the ability to effectively communicate because Lydia said it all for us.  She would cry and say sorry and express that she did not do that on purpose.  She just could not help.

Actually, most of time, we enjoyed her wit and often amazed how quick she could talk and how quick she understood what people trying to say, and the way she said was always so logical.  She could debate so well, that I often did not have any more to say, because her logic was right, and I had to agree with her.

Now, not only it’s too quiet at home.  We found we had difficulty in the communication among us.  And that got a lot of tension.  We realized Lydia used to be facilitator, the mediator of the entire family.  Now, Esther would be the best to do what Lydia used to do.  However, she is too old to talk more than she wanted to.  Being so frustrated, I just missed Lydia so much.  Things are so different without Lydia.

 

Priscilla had to plead somebody to sleep with her every night.  She does not like to sleep alone.  Mom did it several times for last couple weeks.  During the Labor day long weekend, she hoped Esther or Deborah could sleep with her.  Esther really did not like the idea.  Deborah spent two nights with her, and Priscilla was happy.  But it was still not as good, since Deborah fell asleep right away.  Lydia usually would talk to Priscilla until Priscilla fell asleep.  Lydia took care of Priscilla so well, none of us, including mom and dad could do as good job as Lydia did.

This afternoon, Priscilla wanted to ride a bike.  She got on Lydia’s bike, the size was better for Priscilla.  When Lydia was alive, Priscilla had to ride the smaller one, it’s really too small for her.  Now, although Priscilla got the right size, but nobody was riding with her.  So mom did.  And I was fixing the helmet for her.  It took me quite some time, I believe Lydia could do that for Priscilla if she were still alive.

 

Can we not think about Lydia?  It was apparently not possible.

 

When the tension became high today when we could not communicate well, it just came to us that during Lydia’s illness, she kept reminding us to be nice to each other.  Back then, we would calm down right away because she said so, (and she was definitely right).

 

Now Lydia went to be with the Lord, we realized how precious she was, and how much we need to improve ourselves.

 

Once thing important, we got to appreciate each other while we still can.
