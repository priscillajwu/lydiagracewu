March 9, 06

My dear prayer heroes,

Thank you for your prayer. Today, Lydia has gone through another surgery, the insertion of the port for the chemo therapy and spinal fluid sample extraction.

We were told that these two were pretty simple, and we thought it was something very quick and easy. Well, it was much less complex, compared with brain surgery, and we came home the same day. However, she was still under a lot of pain, and suffered some mental distress where she was in the recovery room with us this evening.

Lydia talked to us a lot while she was trying to recover from anesthesia. A lot of them really made my heart ache... She was constantly crying and continuous expressing her discomfort and not feeling well…

"I wish I were a regular kid, so I don’t have to go through all of these…"

"I feel so bad that sisters can’t do as much as they want to do because of me..."

"I would die if any of my sister got brain tumor, it causes the family so much…"

"I want the entire brain radiation treatment. I would give up my left ear hearing. I don’t want to come back again to endure all of these."

"I would like to have icy smoothie if I have dry mouth radiation side effect…, but you have already spent so much money because of me. I feel really bad. You can use my own money to buy…."

"I feel really bad that I have more share of food, and sisters were not happy about it…."

"Are you mad of me? I feel like I screwed up something big…."

I could not remember all of them. I was surprised that these were all in her little mind. While she was weak mentally and physically (resulted from the operation and anesthesia effects?) She would cry and express her feeling. Oh, Lord, please help us and Lydia. Help her to understand deep inside that it’s not her fault. Lord, use her life as the witness of your grace and mercy.

Earlier today, in the clinic check up. Many doctors would jam into the small check up room to take a peek of her. Many were amazed of what she could say. One social worker made a comment, "Lydia is like a star, everybody wanted to see her…" Oh, Lord, please use her to glorify your name.

One thing to mention, we got into preparation room before noon today, we did not get into operation room until 4:30 PM. It was a long wait. Many earlier operations have taken longer time to finish. Also, there was an emergency needed to go before us. We recalled our own two brain surgeries probably causing people after us waiting for a long time as well. While we wait, we understood and were not particularly troubled with the extra long wait. Every sick kid should receive the best care they could have. We could wait a little bit, since our conditions were not as bad as others today in the operation rooms.

God is good, all the time. And may God bless you all.

In Him

Simon
