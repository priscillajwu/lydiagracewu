September 23, 2006, Saturday

Priscilla had mild fever and headache today.  It brought back the memory we had with Lydia in February.

First of all, all four girls rarely got sick.  I do not recall Lydia really got sick in recent years.  She got headache seven months ago, we thought it was just cold or strap throat.  Actually, she had cancer, and died six and half months later.

I was a bit scared.  What happened to us, did make me scare.  Mom was still doing fine.  She called the doctor and got strap throat test ordered, and took Priscilla to the hospital near home (the same place Mom took Lydia many times for blood tests, and other tests.  Not the main hospital.)  Got there, mom was filling the paper work, and not aware that she put down Lydia’s name and birthday.  She just got so used to it.  Then, she realized the mistake and changed it to Priscilla’s.  Of course, this made her very sad and tearful.

Priscilla had strap throat.  They got the antibiotics and came home.  Priscilla took the medicine and was soon feeling better.

Most of kids got sick were like that.  They soon got better.  Lydia could be like that too.  However, that was not the plan God had for Lydia.  Not for mom and dad either.

Lydia seldom got sick since she was born.  Then, her first more serious sickness was life threatening and at the end, it took her life.

 

Life is precious, and also very fragile.  Everything is in God’s hand.  God willing, then we can go to sleep and wake up tomorrow.

 

 

Please continue sharing Lydia’s story.  If you know somebody who is terminally ill, please share Lydia’s story to them.  Tell them that Lydia always has hope, she never gives up to the last minutes.  Yet, if the patients (hopefully the elderly people and had enjoyed the full life) are pretty sure God is taking him or her.  Then, please tell them, there is a winsome girl in heaven waiting for them, as long as they have repented and received Christ Jesus as their savior.  They will be very happy to know her (I felt jealous now.)  Please say hi to Lydia for us…
