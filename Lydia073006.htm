July 30, 2006, Sunday

It was the end of summer swimming season. She has been swimming every year, the same as all her siblings. This summer, while all her same age teammates and all her siblings were practicing and racing, Lydia was in and out of hospital, suffering from severe side effects of chemotherapy, struggling to go through everyday.

Yesterday was the final swim meet with the entire league. Determined and wished to keep this annual event, she wanted to go pretty bad. Yesterday morning, she went and swam with all her strength. The only bold head girl swimmer, she was cheered and applauded by many swimmers, coaches, and parents (many standing). When she got out of water with both arms held by mom, there was a smile on her pale face. As the father, I tried to video tape it. However, I did not get a good shot. I was too emotional at the moment. My eyes were blurred with too much water (tears), and my hand was probably shaking…

As Esther shared yesterday, the true hero can come in different form.

Our little hero was in good mood yesterday. However, she has been a suffering patient today. The headaches were so severe. None of her pain medicines would do any good to relieve her pain. That was very rare. Even more rare is that she could not even watched TV. We were out of means to help her. Again, we almost brought her to ER at night.

We put Lydia to the bed. All of us (mom, dad, and all the sisters) massaged her, on her head, legs, feet, belly… We surrounded her, more than seven or eight hands worked on her at the same time. It must be pretty comfortable. She was able to fall asleep eventually. It marks the end of this weekend, with very highs and lows.

May the name of the Lord be praised.
