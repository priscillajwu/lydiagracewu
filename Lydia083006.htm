August 30, 2006, Wednesday

Compared with yesterday, for whatever reason, mom had much harder time to get through today.

I guess I never expected that life would be this difficult after Lydia passed away. We miss her so much. We woke up in the morning thinking of her. Throughout the day, Lydia is constantly in our minds. At night, we could not sleep because our minds were full of many things about her. However, weeping badly (mom and dad together with door closed) did help us to fall asleep eventually.

I believe God will continue help to get us through. The Lord did carry us through the last two weeks of Lydia’s life on earth. It was so difficult back then. We fast, prayed, and cried. It seemed impossible to get through each day then. But with God’s help, we did make through it. I do not know how long the grieving will be. I’d better hold on to the Lord tightly.

While I am hoping things could slow down a little bit, the world is still going on its own speed. It takes extra effort from me to catch up. Sometimes, it’s hard for me to breathe, I had to remind myself to breathe. Otherwise I may stop breathing for something invisible is pressing me very heavy. Reluctantly, I realized that life goes on outside the windows.

There was an open house of Deborah’s junior high school today. Originally, both mom and dad planned to attend. But, mom just could not make it. She was not feeling well emotionally and physically. When I was there, surrounded by many happy and excited parents, I did not feel easy being there. However, I had to do what I need to do. I made sure all Deborah’s teachers knew what happened recently (actually they already knew). They all thought Deborah have been doing fine so far. Praise the Lord.

I also felt sad that since several years ago, we realized that this year and next year, we would have four kids in four schools (high, junior high, middle, and elementary schools). We were going to be busy hopping around schools. We were eager for this year to arrive. Well, obviously, God thought four schools are too much for us. We could only have three schools like previous years. No need to worry about the middle school, since Lydia did not have opportunity to attend it.

Most people do not like being around with people who grieve all day long. The world today has very little patience for people who mourn. The best comfort I could find is in the Lord Jesus (Matthew 5:4), the body of Christ.  Thank you very much for your prayers and supports.
