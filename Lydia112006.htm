November 20, 2006, Monday

Thanksgiving and Christmas are coming closer and closer.  I am a little bit scared of it.  Not because I don’t like holidays, it’s because the happy memories of Lydia of the past during the holidays brought so much sorrow and pain.

I’ve experienced it before…

The first Chinese New year after my brother passed away (when he and I were both teenagers), was a nightmare.  Entire family mourned terribly.  It was extremely difficult.

We received a long letter from Florida today.  A dear sister in Christ we’ve known for years wrote to us.  We were comforted from her sharing.  She also pointed out that my previous grief of my brother, could make my current grief of Lydia a lot harder.  The sorrow would be deeper.

That could be true.  However, I pray to God to help me.  He is the God of mercy and comfort.  And I am thankful to be able to have God’s grace be with us.

 

In the same way, to plan a vacation is again impossible.  A VACATION WITHOUT LYDIA?  What would it be like?  Can I stand it?

Only to pack the luggage would be an extreme challenge, since Lydia was very good at packing.  She would make sure she got everything, and so did everybody else in the family.  Watching her prepare for the vacation, added so much joy and fun.

“Daddy, did you bring…?”

“Mom, we need to have…”

“Priscilla, do not forget bring the new game…”

She would keep going over whatever she could think of with a lot of excitement.  Mom and I would look at her and laugh.  She was so cute and thoughtful.

 

It’s very difficult to think and continue writing it…

It’s now time to pray for God’s mercy and comfort.  We need strength from the Lord, sound oddly, to get through the holidays, or next family vacation.

 

No fear of holidays, God will help us.  Lydia’s faith of the Lord will always remind and inspire me.
