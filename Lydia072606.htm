July 26, 2006, Wednesday

"I can not stand it any more…" Lydia said it again and again. No exaggeration, probably hundreds of times today.

She was under tremendous suffering for the entire day, both physically and emotionally. She had headache, itchy skin all over her body, stomach ache, numbing leg, and feet, and of course, extremely nauseous all the time. She was not able to walk normally. She cried, screamed, crawled on the floor, and said something very weird, something a normal person would not say. Something she said either made us very sad, or angry, or even scared.

Toward midnight, she was on the verge of giving up everything. We prayed and prayed and prayed. She would slap herself on the head, on her body, on her legs. Looking at her, I think, if it’s me under so much pain and suffering, would I rather die… That was exactly what she was thinking (according to mom, while I was not home, Lydia said that quite a few times).

Oh, Lord, why so much suffering. Can you, the almighty God, relieve our burden a little bit…  I pray.
