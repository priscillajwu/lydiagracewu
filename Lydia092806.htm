September 28, 2006, Thursday

Received today from Lydia’s teacher, who is also currently one of Priscilla’s teachers at elementary school.

“……

There is so much I want to share.  There is a lot that we have already talked about together. 
Here is something you probably know about Lydia-

Anytime someone was negative about their ability, Lydia would hear NONE of it.  She would always stay cheerful and point out the good aspects.  If they were struggling on a brainteaser or project, she would stop what she was doing and help them.  Her help might come in the way of coaching them with really great questions (she always honored the rule of never taking away struggle by telling an answer).  Or she might just be a cheerleader and encourage them, giving them a little pep talk so they weren't feeling so bad.  No matter what she never gave up on people and would not let them give up either.

Today Priscilla came to me with a problem.  She just couldn't  solve one last word puzzle.  Could she stop and go on to the next page?  I asked if I could share something about her sister from last year.  She wanted to hear and I shared with her Lydia's attitude toward perseverance, even on little puzzles.  "Would she let you give up?"  That was all it took.  She headed back to the table and kept at it.  What a wonderful smile when she solved it- she worked it through, had perseverance, and gained a little more faith in her ability to DO IT!

Lydia's spirit is still alive and helping others. 

……”

 

Another one received a few days ago, also from a teacher, who was a few hundreds miles away,

“……

My sister shared your website with me and I have been reading it since July.  I have shared it with my three 10th grade health classes at F.V. High School during our unit on cancer.  There is something about reading about cancer and putting a face on it that impacts us, and I wanted my students to read about Lydia and your family's strength and spirituality.  Take care and God Bless, and thank you for sharing.

……”

 

Thank you for sharing with us.  We are also inspired.

 

 

Today, around noon time, at work, I had to rush to the restroom from my office.  Not because I needed to urinate, but I needed to wash my face and my eyes.  I don’t know what happened.  All of sudden, I had very tearful eyes.  I walked really fast with my head facing down.  One hand covered my nose, and the other hand somewhat covered my eyes.  I don’t think I came across anybody on the hallway.  Otherwise, it would be kind of embarrassing…  Maybe people would think it’s the allergy season.

 

 

I have been sharing with mom these days.  We fear God extremely nowadays…  VERY

Although, we do have to trust God more than ever; otherwise, we won’t be able to survive.  However, the fear is very intense.  (Job 23:13-17)
