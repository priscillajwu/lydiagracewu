September 20, 2006, Wednesday

Priscilla came home with the letter from school, the first paragraph,

Dear Symmes Community,

 

Many of you have been touched by Lydia Wu’s extraordinary life, and have been searching for a way to honor her memory.  For those of you unaware, Lydia was diagnosed with brain cancer in February 2006 and passed away on August 21, 2006.  Lydia attended Symmes Elementary from first through fourth grade, and would have been in fifth grade at Greene School this year.  In her time at Symmes, she excelled in many areas including acting in the Symmes Players Spring production, Honk, Jr., and winning the Fourth Grade Spelling Bee, both completed after her diagnosis…

 

The school PTO board decided to purchase an outdoor musical instrument, which reflects Lydia’s love of music, with a plaque mounted on it to commemorate Lydia.

Reading the letter made me cry.  Lydia has been so loved by many. 

I missed her so much.  Even a wish to hug her again or hear her speak again is impossible.

 

The girls were back to tennis lesson today, of course, without Lydia.  The thought again made me sad.  I could recall the picture she played tennis not long before she passed away.  Not like her siblings, she played seriously and appreciated the opportunity every single time.  Yes, that was her.  Not take for granted for a lot of things.

 

Today, the Lord reminded me the Scriptures, Philippians 1:21-24.

For to me, to live is Christ and to die is gain. If I am to go on living in the body, this will mean fruitful labor for me. Yet what shall I choose? I do not know! I am torn between the two: I desire to depart and be with Christ, which is better by far; but it is more necessary for you that I remain in the body.

 

I tried to take easy and prayed whenever the emotion got me.  It helped a little.  Many friends have reminded us to take care of ourselves for three girls, and for the Lord, and that would be what Lydia likes.

Thank you for the prayers, love, and support.

May God bless you and may your faith with the Lord grow everyday.
