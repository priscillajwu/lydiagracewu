June 13, 2006, Tuesday

Ever since chemotherapy started yesterday, Lydia has been urinating every 15 to 20 minutes. Therefore, she did not have much sleep since yesterday.

Today, her nausea got much worse. Although we got all the anti-nauseous medicines to her, she is still feeling very nauseous.

At night, I spent hours to feed her, little by little, sip by sip. Then, she threw up everything. Not giving up, we tried again, I took a few hours to get her eat something, several small bites of chicken, some potato, and drank some soda. I stood on her bedside, with food on my hand. She ate very very slow. Then, all of sudden, she threw up all of them again…

Lydia kept saying sorry that she did not do it on purpose.

"It’s OK. It’s not your fault." I said with a deep sadness in my heart.

My heart aches seeing her under so much suffering.

Lydia lost almost another pound since yesterday.

We knew the chemotherapy might be difficult. Now we are experiencing it, it’s hard to describe the level of frustration and sorrow.

Please pray for the doctors so they have wisdom and know how to get Lydia’s conditions under control as soon as possible.

We need the strength from the Lord. May God’s mercy and grace be upon us.
