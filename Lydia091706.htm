September 17, 2006, Sunday

This afternoon, I attended church board meeting which I missed last month due to Lydia was in critical condition.

I recalled that a few times during Lydia’s illness.  She would remind me,

“Daddy, you should go to the church board meeting.”  Lydia said.

“Why, don’t you want me to stay home to be with you and take care of you?”  I said to her.

“I think you should go.”  Lydia would always say, “It’s important.”

Indeed, when she was in terrible condition or dying.  Nothing was more important, nothing.  To her, only things directly related to God’s kingdom were important. 

We can argue that many things were indirectly related to God.  But, come on, we know it.  What is important in God’s eye, what is not?  Often, we only look for our own enjoyment.

 

After meeting, I went to Lydia’s grave and wept for a long time.  I wept because I missed Lydia so much.  I wept because many people do not take life seriously.  They took for granted being alive.  Sometimes, I felt Lydia has died in vain.

 

I need to work hard to use Lydia’s life to help people.  Life is so precious.  To live is to honor God, our Creator.  Please pray for us.  There were many suggestions from friends.  We need to ask for God’s wisdom and guidance.

 

At the end of Lydia’s life, she knew what was important, not something most kids would think of, like having fun, or having good toy or entertainment.  She knew only things for God were important.  Many other things were trivial.

“I don’t care those things.”  She often said that.

When she said that, we were troubled.  We thought she lost the interest in many things in life.  In fact, she could only focus on the important things, and those things are truly important, with eternal value, not for earthly value.
