September 22, 2006, Friday

Thank you for sending us the words of comfort, encouragement, love, faith, and hope.  We have shared some at Lydia’s farewell celebration party.  Many told us they were very touched by reading the sharings from friends, uncles and aunts, and brothers and sisters.

Here is the excerpt of email I received today…

……

I try to share Lydia's message as often as I can.  N.H. Christian Church's college group , the other church I attend, led a Bible study based on Lydia's story.  I couldn't stay because I cried too much…but many people were amazed.  I heard that the lesson greatly changed some people and many understood God better because of it.  I know NOTHING can make-up for your loss here on earth…but I really believe that God has a big plan.  He has always been in control…. From the moment death entered the world through our sin.

I know no one feels the loss of Lydia like you do.

However, Lydia's death greatly pains me.  I would start crying at work, in front of my friends, and at home.  I also have dreams about her (more than once).  I wanted to share one dream with you very quickly.  I dreamed she came back to life and looked as if she was in her teen years.  She had a very beautiful face…and I suspect she was not on earth but in Heaven.  She was upset we were all in tears and told us again and again, "Did you not expect me to rise from the dead?"  She was so flustered, and she laughed when she said this…like we were being crazy for not content with the fact that she has risen. 

I have learned so much from Lydia and her patience, perseverance, and faith…

……

Thank you, my sister in the Lord, Christ Jesus.

 

 

I saw a few Lydia’s good friends tonight at church.  They were fifth grade children, mostly ten years old, still the age of innocence, and full of happiness.

I recalled Lydia has grown a lot during her last half year life on earth, not only her faith, but also her characters, personality, even her look.  Her face features looked more mature than her age should look, like a teenager.

Indeed, suffering does make us grow.  Lydia’s last half year of suffering probably made her grow up several years in many ways, especial her love for the Lord…

 

 

I shared Philippians 1:23 again tonight in the bible study.

“I desire to depart and be with Christ, which is better by far.”

I was very familiar with this verse.  But I only comprehended it in a logical way.  But now I truly KNOW it.

I was surprised that all my children (Esther, Deborah, Priscilla, and of course, Lydia) KNOW this deep in their hearts.

“I admire your faith, girls.”  I told Esther and Deborah at night (Priscilla has gone to sleep), “You and I were raised up differently.”

Praise the Lord for the faith we all have now.  And indeed it’s very important to raise our children in the Lord, with faith, love, and hope.
