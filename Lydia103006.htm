October 30, 2006, Monday

I was not feeling well in the evening, with stomachache and sorrowful mind.  I lay down on the sofa, thinking how come life continues to be difficult…

Then, I recalled that during Lydia’s illness, many times, she told me, “Daddy, it’s so hard...”

I could see from her face that she was in extreme suffering, pain, and discomfort.

Thought of that, only brought more tears to my eyes.  Lydia’s hardship was probably hundreds or thousands times more difficult than what I am suffering now, considering she was only a nine years old girl…

Inspired, I got up, slowly moved to the computer.  Open up the email, a poem sent to us from a sister in Christ. 

http://www.homewithgod.com/Cards/holdon.shtml

Thanks for encouragement, my dear sister.

 

Another daily bread I received today,

http://dailywisdom.gospelcom.net/archives/2006/10/28/index.html#001993

At the end, the author concluded,

Our time here on earth is so short. That time also tends to go very fast as we involve ourselves in our families, career, social life and hobbies. The reality is that we do not know when sickness, disease, or unexpected accident may occur.

Do not put off a relationship with God and His son, Jesus Christ, until time of tragedy. Today is the day to create new hope and be assured through faith of your heavenly reward.

Please share with your loved ones, who tended to put off the decision to follow Jesus, and hopefully Lydia’s short life could give them some urgency and willingness.  Then, it will bring us a lot of comfort.

May the name of the Lord be praised.
