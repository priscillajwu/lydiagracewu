October 9, 2006, Monday

Over last week, we were told and also read from the emails sent to us that some decided to accept the Lord Christ Jesus at the evangelical meeting held last week have been following Lydia’s story and were touched.

One parent mentioned that, he loves his children very much.  From Lydia’s story, he realized the most important and precious thing he could give to his children is the faith.  He would hope his children have the faith like Lydia’s.  However, if he could not be the role model himself to lead his children to God.  It won’t work well.

One young mother was moved by Lydia’s story.  Lydia’s pure faith made her think.  As a mother of young children, she has deep feeling towards Lydia and could not help but believe in the Lord, who is so real during Lydia’s illness.

In the storm Lydia faced, her faith was all it mattered.  Parents, teachers, doctors, nurses, friends, no one could truly help her or comfort her in the deepest level.  Only Jesus Himself, deep in her heart, IS with her.  That was why to the very end, Lydia still has peace and hope, and NO FEAR.  Isn’t that amazing.

Yes, there will be many difficulties and obstacles in our children’s life.  In the future, there will be suffering, and pain in their life.  What else, what else is more important to lead them to the Lord, to have strong faith and trust in Him.

Praise the Lord that God continues use Lydia’s story to save souls for His kingdom.

 

Having said that, tell the truth, for the last couple months, I have been in lowest moment in my life.  I felt extreme failure and ultimate defeat.  God gave me a wonderful daughter to take care.  And I…, I lost her to the terrible disease.  She died.  I felt so miserable and ashamed of doing a poor job and incapable of saving her.  People would say I don’t have to feel that way, or that is the accusation from the evil one.  Or said that is a normal reaction in the psychological aspect.  All I can say is I need to be humble before the Lord.  I used to think I could do something for the Lord.  NO, what can I do, nothing.  It’s the work of the Holy Spirit.  I can only be a tool for Him.

Although a total failure in my part, God is still in control.  And the Lord gave me comfort, also in the deepest level.  Otherwise, daily life would be a struggle and so difficult.

 

Quite a few worried about my physical health and sent me email or told me I should take care of myself for the family.  Thanks a lot.  I replied them I am fine and doing everything I can to do well.  I know too well that Lydia is in a much better place, and I deeply believe in that.  I long for the same place myself at the end of my days on earth.

But I grieve because I miss Lydia greatly.  It’s that simple.

 

I treasure what Jesus said, Matthew 5:4,

“Blessed are those who mourn, for they will be comforted.”
