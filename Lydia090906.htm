September 9, 2006, Saturday

It’s the weekend again.  On Friday night, without the need to take care of Lydia, (in last seven months, because of Lydia’s health condition, either mom or dad had to stay home with Lydia), we all went to church to serve and attending the bible study.  It’s the first day of children program for the new year (usually after Labor day).  It was very nice to be among brothers and sisters in Christ.  We felt joyful and peaceful.  Praise the Lord.

Lydia enjoyed every Friday night at church so much (she went to church every Friday night since she was one month old).  She always liked to memorize bible verses, and enjoyed singing hymns, and playing with kids in church.

Saturday afternoon, a couple of Lydia’s good friends invited Priscilla over their house to play.  Priscilla had a great time.  When mom went to take Priscilla home, she did not want to.  Priscilla said, “There is nobody to play with me.”  That was a tough statement, and made mom very sad.  Yes, we miss Lydia so much.

Everything in daily life still made me think about Lydia.  This afternoon, I was home alone, and took a look of Lydia’s diary and pictures.  I was very tired, and would like to take a nap.  However, I could not fall asleep.  There was some uneasiness and heavy sadness in me.  I felt like I was real sick and my head was really heavy.  My brain was not resting, many thoughts running through my mind.  I have to get up and walk around in the house until kids came back.  Interestingly, before, I always enjoyed the time alone and got something done or took a good nap (with four daughters, having sometime alone was very nice), but now, I rather have them to surround me all the time.

Many people told us to take time to grieve.  Well, grieving is not enjoyable, and it’s very difficult to endure.  I think the best way to deal with grieving is to focus on God, focus on the ministries, and focus on the people with needs.

 

Would like to share one Scripture,

“And the God of all grace, who called you to his eternal glory in Christ, after you have suffered a little while, will himself restore you and make you strong, firm and steadfast.”  1 Peter 5:10.

I am longing for this to happen soon.
