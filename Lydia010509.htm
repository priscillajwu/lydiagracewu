January 5, 2009, Monday

 

So, it’s another year.  My true feeling is that last year has gone by so slow.  I wish it could speed up more.

 

Why is the sorrow still so fresh and painful?  In the morning of the new years day, I wished I could wake up and everything changed magically.  Lydia could be still around and no more tears on my cheek and in my soul…

However, I woke up and found myself grieving terribly.  Pain and sorrow are still there, not any less.  I am still facing so many difficulties in life.  The problems are still there and did not disappear, waiting for me to solve, to take care, to overcome, to get over.

 

Why is life so difficult?  Actually, I do know the answer.  This is what God has in store for me.  But knowing the answer does not give me the strength and wisdom.  It’s so difficult to continue surviving.  Then, I realized what I need to do, knelt down and prayed very hard immediately.  And God helped, at that very morning of the new years day.

 

A movie star’s son died accidently.  All the news were all over about what happened.  It was not too wise for me to follow the news.  It was very difficult.  I read an article in CNN full of the descriptions from the experts,

“Losing a child is like losing the center of your life.”

“The loss of a child is the most painful loss that humans can sustain.”

“Our job as a parent is to protect and raise them, look out for them, anticipate their needs.  When a child dies, your brain just works to try to make sense out of nonsense. It's not supposed to have happened. It's a big reason why the pain is so acute and long lasting.”

….

 

I know all that.  It does not help by reading these articles.  It only gave me even more sorrow and pain.

 

I then realized again I needed to pray.  The prayers are a lot more helpful.

 

I pray God to help me to have peace.  Then, I pray for the actor’s family.  It’s surely very painful to see other people grieving like me.  Nobody should deserve this kind of pain.  But if God allowed this to happen, then we need a lot of prayers.

 

“The peace of God, which transcends all understanding, will guard your hearts, and your minds in Christ Jesus.”  Philippians 4:7.
