September 5, 2006, Tuesday

Every morning, mom would wake up Priscilla and held her really tight before school.  This morning Mom asked her, “Do you miss Lydia?”

“Yes, a lot.  I dreamed of Lydia that she was here playing with me.  It’s so real.  Then, I woke up and realized it’s just a dream.”  Priscilla said both sadly and happily.

Mom was a little bit jealous.  She wished so bad that she could have a good dream about Lydia, but so far, none.  Most of her dreams were still full of stress and nightmares.

Well, it’s been a nightmare for mom and dad.  We woke up into the nightmare.  The reality is like a nightmare.  Only when we were in sleep, then we did not feel sorrowful.

 

Today, there was school curriculum review from Priscilla’s elementary school, which Lydia spent last four years of her life (from first grade to fourth grade), and with a lot of memories there.

Mom could not do it.  So I had to go.  Seeing Lydia’s teachers, and many parents of Lydia’s friends really made me sad.  When I left, I stopped by the hallway where the graduated class’s group photo was displayed.  I saw Lydia there, in the picture, with big smile.  That picture was taken not long before she got ill.  All of these were still so fresh.  It seemed not long ago, I was there for Lydia’s curriculum review (one year ago).  One year later, Lydia is no longer with us…  Please appreciate our loved ones, for life is so short.  For some, it’s even shorter, way too short.

I walked to the parking lot of the school, glanced the playground which Lydia had a lot of laughs and funs there (especially monkey bars).  Many tears were already on my face.  I rushed into the car, and drove away quickly so that I could cry loudly and nobody could see me.

 

During the same time I was in the school curriculum review, Mom took Priscilla to Lydia’s grave.  They prepared a lot of flowers.  Priscilla had a lot of fun there.  She spent so much time there and did not want to leave.  She said she had good time there arranging flower around Lydia’s grave…

 

Mom got sick today.  It’s really the first time in seven months.  For the past seven months, whenever mom did not feel good, Lydia would pray for mom, and mom would feel better right away.  Both of them knew that mom could not get sick, since she needed to take care of Lydia.

 

All the memories about Lydia actually hurt.  Many cards we received mentioned that the sweet memories we have with Lydia would comfort us.  That seems not the case.  Every memory about Lydia made us cry and heart ache.  We just miss her too much.

 

At night, mom asked Priscilla again, “Do you miss Lydia?”

“Yes, but I know she is in a much better place.  She deserves the best…”  Priscilla said it without any doubt in her expression.
