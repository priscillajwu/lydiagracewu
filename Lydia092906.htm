September 29, 2006, Friday

While going through everyday continues to be a struggle for me, It seems to me that I am rather weak in many ways.

A thought came to me today, “This is not Lydia’s way.”

Recall even during her final stage of illness, she still LIVED STRONG.  So strong, now that I thought of it, I felt ashamed.

Only ten years old, Lydia, was so optimistic about life, so eager to do everything in her life, she was always ready to go.  I, as her father, even have a lot of things to learn from her.

Did I influence her?  Maybe, in some ways.  However, a lot of her strengths were probably from God directly, and because of the prayers of many during her illness…

 

 

We could not find things a lot more often than when Lydia was still around.  Some of just could not remember where we put things.  Lydia was always able to find the stuff for us.  She got good ideas and clues all the time.

“I got a good idea.”  She said that a lot when she was younger.

She seemed to be the one always knew what’s going on around her.  She would remind us what we forgot.  She knew where we put things.  She was always so alert, and constantly reminding, and helping.  So, she had to talk a lot.  Oh, I miss her non-stopping talking so much.

 

 

Tonight, we talked about eating at dinner table.  It hurts me a lot…

Deborah and Priscilla are the picky eaters compared with the other two.  Lydia was the one who would eat everything, eat vegetables, and dare to try new stuff, even better than Esther.  I often appraised her that this was the way to prevent diseases or cancer, because there are a lot of anti-oxidant in those vegetables.

Lydia did not ask me why she got cancer that she listened to mom and dad and ate everything on the dinner table.  I would be speechless and very sad if she asked that.

I don’t have answer to a lot of questions.  But one thing I can be sure is that God is in control in everything.  God willing, then we can have another day to live, work, eat, and drink, and enjoy God’s creation.

 

A colleague told me a quote,

“People plan, God laughs.”

 

I am not saying we should not plan for the future, but we need to fear God, and seek for His guidance all the time.  And at the end, the results are in His hands.
