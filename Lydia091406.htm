September 14, 2006, Thursday

Mom reminded me about the hair donation.  Actually, Esther and Deborah did donate their hair in summer 2005.  Lydia’s hair was not long enough.  Lydia said back then, she would like to donate her hair in summer 2006.  Well, sadly Lydia lost all her hair before she could donate, and too bad she did not live long enough to receive one either.

 

We noticed that Priscilla’s math skills have improved quite a bit recently.  Thanks to Lydia, for she taught Priscilla all summer long, either at home or at hospital, on the sofa or on the hospital bed.  Now, although Lydia is gone, Priscilla has caught up some of Lydia’s math wit.  (Lydia was taking sixth grade math when she was in fourth grade.)

Mom asked Priscilla how come she would make such a progress (last year, Priscilla was not very good at math at all.)

“Because I feel Lydia is with me all the time.”  Priscilla said.  This just made mom so tearful.

 

Mom accidentally drove her car into the home garage door today.  When mom called me and asked me what to do, “What would Lydia react?” first came to my mind.  I am sure Lydia would say, “That is OK, not a big deal.”  Indeed, compared with what happened to her and the family.  This is truly nothing.  It’s not worthy to be upset about the door or the car.  As long as nobody got hurt, then we should be thankful.

Thinking of Lydia has taught me and reminded me in many ways.

 

When I got home, mom looked at me and asked,

“Did you go to Lydia’s grave?”

“Yeah, how do you know?”  I asked.

“By looking at your eyes.”  Mom said.

I thought I’ve wiped out all the tears.  However, my eyes must be so swollen that she could notice it right away.

 

Lydia’s stuff are still all over the house.  Mom can only did a little bit a day.  To clean up Lydia’s stuff is very very difficult task to mom.  Actually, I am even worse, I could not do it at all.  I would burst into tears right away.  I suggested mom to get her friends over to help.  She said she rather do it by herself, little by little.

 

The suffering and pain is so great.  I grieve day and night.  Each night, going to bed is really tough for me, since I could get nightmare or very stressful dreams.  However, it’s still better to be sleeping, because every morning, I woke up into the worst nightmare, the reality, that Lydia is no longer with us…

 

 

At night, Mom said Lydia is the luckiest among us.  It is so tough to be left behind.  I totally agreed.  However, eventually, we will all get together with the Lord, sooner or later.  If, at the end, still get left behind, it would be worse than the worst nightmare I could ever imagine.

 

Praise the Lord, we have ultimate hope while we miss Lydia.  I truly don’t know how can people survive without this hope.  Are they take a chance or they don’t care?  Or they just have super strong mind or philosophy to help them?  I think the best answer is that God is still waiting for them to turn to God…
