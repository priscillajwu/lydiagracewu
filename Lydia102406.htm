October 24, 2006, Tuesday

We would like to thank many friends helping distributing Lydia’s bracelet.  There were some requests from out of town.  Mom will start to mail them.  Thank you for continuing sharing Lydia’s story.

Several bible study group leaders shared with us that everybody in their group wore the bracelet.  We received one picture today from a group of church ladies.  It’s so lovely.  Thank you, it brought us smile and comfort.

 

A brother in Christ shared with me last Sunday that he agrees that there will be adversity and suffering happen to everybody, in different forms.  It’s not “if”, but “when”.

The problem is, are we ready yet?

We thank God that Lydia, since she was very young, has strong faith of the Lord, love for the people, and unfailing hope.  We all should be like her.

 

A very close friend of us, has suffered serious headache lately.  Her doctor does not think it’s serious.  Knowing what happened to Lydia, she was a bit of worried.  Mom shared with her,

“It could be just stress.  Even if there is something serious, to be with the Lord is great.  It is very difficult for those who left behind.”

That is very true.  When (not if, it’s just matter of time) I got terminally ill, I will be thinking about Lydia, I will have great comfort, hope, and yes, you bet, JOY, that I will be re-united with Lydia and meet the Lord soon.  How nice.

 

“For to me, to live is Christ and to die is gain.” Philippians 1:21.

Lydia’s life was like this verse described.  Our life should be like that too.
