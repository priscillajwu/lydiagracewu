October 14, 2006, Saturday

It’s the mission month for our church.  An old American missionary, born in China, grew up in India, educated in the US seminary, spent most his life in poor villages of Taiwan and Philippine, came to share his story, with God’s glory and amazing grace.

He shared that it’s true joy to suffer for the Lord.  Those years in the mission field are happiest and most important in his life.

I was very touched by his sharing.  Indeed, only through the suffering and adversity, we could really experience the true joy and peace.  Apostle Paul reminded us again and again in his letters to the early churches.  It’s true, so true.

This verse came to me afterward,

“…you shine like stars in the universe as you hold out the word of life…” Philippians 2:15.

 

Many shared with us that Lydia’s life is like a little shining star.  This is from couple days ago,

“…Ever since Esther posted that Lydia wanted her Light to shine and that her favorite Bible song is this little Light of Mine, I think of Lydia when I hear the word "light". This passage is seemingly appropriate because Lydia seemed to have a special quality that allowed her to excel with the gifts she was given for her life on earth and she was brave enough to celebrate her gifts of swimming, tennis, spelling, music, finding lost things, gabbing, and the many things you remark upon…”

Lydia, my dear daughter, my little shining star, I miss you more than I could express.  It’s just so…, so difficult to go on without you around…
