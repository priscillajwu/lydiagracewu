November 9, 2008, Sunday

Girls had their hair cut today.

It is the second time they had hair cut after Lydia passed away.  The hair will be donated to “Locks of Love”. 

I know many girls keep the hair long enough and then have it cut and donated to the organization like Locks of Love for the cancer patients.  Well, there is difference here.  We have pure black hair and that is the hair color we could not get when Lydia needed a wig with the black color.

Although Lydia could not get the black color hair wig, hopefully with Lydia’s sisters’ multiple donations.  There will be the black color hair wig (the true hair, not artificially made) available now for the cancer patients.

 

We do not know who will be the recipient, probably a girl or a lady, of the hair we donated.  We pray that God has mercy on her and her treatment to be successful.  And she has the courage to fight, never give up, and live the life to the fullest.

 

 

 

Since Lydia’s book published in Chinese a couple months ago, quite a few people told us about the impact they had when reading Lydia’s story.  It’s very comforting when we heard that.  The inspiration is from the Lord.  We praise God. 

Although we could not bring Lydia back from the death, we will look forward, and do whatever we need to fulfill this life.  The death is the certain destiny for everyone.  We need to make sure God is happy about our life when we reach the end of our days.  That day is either one day from now or decades from now.  And that should not make any difference how we live today.
