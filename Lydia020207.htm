February 2, 2007, Friday

“Is your work really busy recently?  Are you stressed?”  A friend asked me.

“Yes, we have a lot of things going on at work with very tight schedule.  And I work a lot of hours too.” I replied, “However, I don’t feel stressed from the work.  I can not feel it.”

“Oh, that means you have something more stressful going on.”

“Yes, I am in deep sorrow constantly.”  I said.

 

Indeed, on top of the daily work of my job.  I have been in various church meetings every evening this week.  And three girls have their busy schedules too.  A lot of transportation needs.  However, I don’t feel stressed from these things, peak work demands, church responsibilities, and more than regular chores…

I feel numb on some of those things.  And I feel numb physically too, arms and legs, resonant with the emotions.

What I like most nowadays are meditation, reading, praying, and of course, thinking about Lydia, throughout the day, and many late nights…

 

Is a long life on earth a blessing or a curse?  I am not sure.  It depends.  But one thing I am sure is in Luke 12:16-21.

 

“The ground of a certain rich man yielded an abundant harvest.  He thought to himself, 'What shall I do? I have no place to store my crops.'

"Then he said, 'This is what I'll do. I will tear down my barns and build bigger ones, and there I will store my surplus grain.  And I'll say to myself, "You have plenty of grain laid up for many years. Take life easy; eat, drink and be merry." '

"But God said to him, 'You fool! This very night your life will be demanded from you. Then who will get what you have prepared for yourself?'

"This is how it will be with those who store up things for themselves but are not rich toward God.”
