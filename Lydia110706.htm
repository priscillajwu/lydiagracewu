November 7, 2006, Tuesday

Deborah had a dentist appointment today.  Recalled Lydia’s courage, no numbing medicine.  Since Lydia started requesting no numbing medicine, now all three girls can do the same thing.

 

 

Mom bought a book today about a twin brother and sister, the brother died from cancer, and the sister told the story.

From the book, after her brother passed away, she learned the lesson from support group that, grieving takes time.  How much time? I really wondered.

 

“For many cancer patients, during their illness, they found inspiration from other people to help them go through.”  Mom told me while reading through the book, “But Lydia was different.  She shined with her own characters.”

“Yeah, that’s true.” I said, “I never realized that.”

No wonder Lydia is so amazing.  She truly has been the miracle from the Lord, and lived within us.  Just too short.

 

I recalled the quote from Priscilla, “Lydia is the girl who lives, and the girl who has faith.”

Yes, Lydia lives, as long as her story continues to be told and shared...

May the name of the Lord be glorified.
