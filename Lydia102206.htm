October 22, 2006, Sunday

A special mission month Sunday worship today.  Several parents of the young missionaries came to share with us.

They mentioned the struggle when their sons decided to become a missionary, and the joy and honor now that their sons are serving the Lord.

One young missionary went to pre-medical school after high school in the first place, then decided to go to seminary.  Because, in his young mind, medical doctor could heal people physically, but the person would still eventually die.  He wanted to be the doctor who could heal people, who will never die.  So, now he is, to save people, to help them to know God, to receive salvation from the Lord, so his patients have eternal life.

This missionary’s mother shared that she was diagnosed with severe cancer one year ago.  She was told she only had two months to live.  Now, almost one year has past, she is pretty sure, the extra time the Lord gave her is continue witnessing the Lord.

With God’s grace, she was able to tour China just two months ago with her younger sisters.  Her sister shared the plan of retirement, and was really excited about it.  Unfortunately, her younger sister passed away just couple weeks ago from a car accident.

“This maybe your first time to meet me, very possible the last time.”  (Because of her illness) The missionary’s mother shared, “But I know where I am going, I am worry about any of you who have not received Christ Jesus...”

 

What a strong testimony.  I know better, that life is so short, we could plan many things about our own future, but we don’t know how many of them can we carry out.  More importantly, it’s to live for God, now, at this moment, because we don’t know if we have tomorrow.

Lydia did not have too many tomorrow, but she lives every moment to the full extent.  We should live everyday like we do not have tomorrow.

 

It would be nice that Lydia would become a missionary herself and went to the mission field to serve the Lord.  Then, I could be the parent of the missionary.

God did not arrange that way.  I need to be missionary myself, I guess.
