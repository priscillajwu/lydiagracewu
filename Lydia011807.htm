January 18, 2007, Thursday

Deborah had her birthday party.  The very first birthday in the family after Lydia passed away.  As usual, we took the family picture in front of the birthday cake.  Only five people in the picture.  Five?  Why Five?  I still do not understand why there are only five people in the picture.  That hurts.  That hurts a lot.

 

I used to think it must be so hard for God to sacrifice His son, and only son for the world.  Now, I know it’s not only hard, it’s so impossible.  I would rather die myself, instead of sacrifice my child.  Nothing can make me give up Lydia.  None.  No matter what.

God’s heart will hurt even more when people ignore, don’t care, or even laugh about it and go on their own way.

 

Sometimes, I would imagine that I grab a non-believer, and shout to him, “Are you blind?  Can you see there is something more there?  The reason there is suffering because there is something more in life and beyond life…”

I am sure this person would politely push me away, and said, “Excuse me” and walked away thinking I am crazy (or stupid).  He would go on to try his best to avoid, to prevent any adversity in life.  If he is so “lucky” or “unfortunate” that no serious enough trouble happen in his life to awake him, and chose to deny the salvation.  Then, at the end, Satan won his soul.

 

Suffering was never welcomed.  People do not appreciate it, or don’t even want to think about it (seems it would bring them bad luck).  Even if it happens to them, many people would numb themselves with other means like addictions (food, work, sin, etc…).

 

I know many people who were very familiar with Lydia’s story but their hearts did not change.

“Hey, it’s Simon’s daughter.  Not mine.  I am sorry for what happen to his family.  (And tell me if I can be of any help.)  But mine is OK.  I don’t think this kind of thing would ever happen to me.  My life is good.  I don’t need God…”

 

So sad, I only hope that Lydia’s story can be like a seed, planted in their heart and hopefully sprout soon, before it’s too late.

 

 

A sister in Christ wrote to us today that she would equip herself to grow faith stronger.

“For my time will come, too.”  She shared.

And that is the wisdom.
