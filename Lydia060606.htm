Dear friends,

 

          “Nice, Lydia! You got a strike! Awesome…” Lydia’s eyes showed happiness and glee as she spent the morning bowling with her fellow 4th graders on their last day of school. Despite everything that she’s been going through, she was adamant about attending the last day of school. Since I didn’t need to go to school, I was her personal bodyguard throughout the entire day. Not only did she want to go bowling; she wanted to ride the bus.

“I haven’t ridden the bus in a long time!” she pleaded. The hot, confined space made me nervous, but she insisted. I sat with her on the bus, as my mom followed the buses in the car. As soon as she joined her friends, they drew her back into their conversations as if there was nothing different. Within seconds they were telling her problems that were going on and the newest pieces of information about the 4th graders. I smiled broadly as I looked on. Often the bus got quite loud and chaotic. When this happened, Lydia would lay her head on my shoulder, and I would put my head on hers, and we’d rest for a little bit. I thanked God for every intimate moment I was able to spend with my little sister.

At first Lydia had a lot of trouble bowling. She couldn’t throw the ball very hard. My mom and I kept encouraging her, however. Soon after she started, she got a spare! All of her friends screamed and cheered loudly. I gave her a huge hug. Then, she got a strike! I was so proud of her. I made sure to take tons of pictures. Later on, back at school, the 4th graders had a pizza party and ordered ice cream from the ice cream truck. Mom tried to convince her to go home, but Lydia would not leave school until the end of the day. She said goodbye to her loving teacher, her best friends and kind classmates. The day, although tiring, was so much fun, and I was so glad that Lydia had a chance to be a normal 4th grader, having fun, and socializing with friends, for just one day.

          2 hours after we got home, it was time to head out again. Our summer swim team was having its first swim meet of the season! And Lydia was going to swim! Many of the members of the swim team asked if Lydia had decided not to attend the meet. Imagine their surprise as I proudly told them that Lydia was here at the meet. Not only did she want to swim; she didn’t want to miss a single one of her sisters’ events, or for that matter, any events. I got a chair for her, and put it right by the pool. Lydia had me sit down, and she sat on my lap, and wouldn’t let me leave. Since being diagnosed, Lydia has been so much more affectionate, and wanting and giving hugs. I loved being close to her and held her and enjoyed the night, as we waited for Lydia’s event.

          “Event 47! Girls’ 9-10 50 meter breast stroke! Step up!” I waited anxiously by the blocks as Lydia got into the pool and started swimming. I screamed and cheered at the top of my lungs as I watched my sister put her heart and everything into swimming those two laps, and she finished strong. She was barely able to climb out of the pool and she was panting heavily. I began crying and hugged her. Lydia, who had feared that she’d never be able to swim again had just competed in a swim meet, and sprinted two whole laps! I was infinitely proud and so amazed. Praise God for what He has done for Lydia! It is truly a miracle what she is able to do.

 

Indeed, today truly was a day of wonders, and I thank God that I was there to witness every moment of it. Every moment of success and pride that God has given to my determined, courageous, and above all, loving little sister.

 

 

Love,

Esther
