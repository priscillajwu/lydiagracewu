November 22, 2006, Wednesday

All three girls did not have school today.  Mom and dad suggested let’s do something fun.  We planned to go to a state park.  I had the driving direction and map ready last night.

This morning, as usual, Mom got up early morning (could not sleep anymore) and mourned for a few hours, waiting for the girls to get up.

What happened was that the younger two did not want to go.  I tried to convince them to get going, but they just did not move at all.  So, we stuck at home.  I was frustrated.

We were not feeling good, actually in sorrow.  I thought going out to the park will help, but the girls did not want to go anywhere.

Later, I realized.  Where is the promoter?  Where is Lydia?

Before, in situation like this, Lydia would get up early in the morning, get herself ready, and then wake up the siblings.  She would be so ready to have fun, and to get outdoors.  The sisters would go along with her.  Lydia had the magic to encourage, to promote, and to get people excited.  She would have enjoyed so much.  People around her would be influenced.

And now, the girls just did not want to move.  I was not in a very good mood.  Nobody liked my plan to have some outdoor fun.  I realized that Lydia was my best supporter.  She liked the activities.  She wanted to do it, to learn, to do her best, TO LIVE IT ALL.

It was truly terrible.  We wanted to go out to feel better, but because we were not feeling good, so we did not want to go out.  How trouble it is.

Mid afternoon, mom and dad commanded everybody got into the car.  We were going out, no matter what.

We went to Lydia’s grave first.  While there, I’ve noticed in many husband and wife’s graves, that the wives usually out lived husband, and some of them by several decades.

“See that, mom.”  I pointed to two tombs with wife died thirty years after her husband.  “Look like I will go first.”  I laughed so loud after seeing quite a few tombs were like that.  I have not laughed so loud in a quite while.  What I have in my mind was that I will get to see Lydia first.  The thought just made me very happy.

“No, I don’t think God will be that good to you.  I will go first.”  Mom also laughed loudly.

Truly, blessed is the one who departs first.

Then, we went to a winery.  They advertised to be the largest and oldest winery in Ohio.  When we tried to decide what sparking juice to buy, I let the girls to decide among themselves.  Then, I noticed another big difference.

Before, the decision was always difficult, always two against two.  Today, it was easy, two against one.  Esther lost.

I am still not used to an odd number yet.  I kept forgetting I have three daughters now, not four…

 

At the end of day, I thank God we had some laughter today.  I know quite a few still keep us in prayers, especially during this holiday season.

Thank you.  It helps.  Praise the Lord.
