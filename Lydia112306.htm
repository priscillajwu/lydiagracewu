November 23, 2006, Thursday

First Thanksgiving without Lydia.

Since we do not have any relatives nearby, in previous years, we would open our house to host friends and brothers and sisters from bible study group to celebrate Thanksgiving.

Tonight, we were able to go to another dear friend’s house.  Many families were there, several dozens people, some old friends, some new friends.  We had good time together sharing God’s love and testimony.  Lydia’s life was again remembered for her faith and courage.

I met a couple today.  Twenty years ago, their youngest son suffered from a tragic accident and became vegetated and stayed in the hospice (he passed away six years ago, 14 years after the accident).  Since then, they left the Lord with anger toward God.  Only until recently, they returned to the church.  The husband shared the pain, the bitterness, the anger, the ultimate sorrow, and to some extent, lost the will to live.  They endured for all these years.  Praise the Lord.  They were back to the Lord’s kingdom.  The reason why they came back is because a friend’s advice to them,

“Brother, you need to be humble before the Lord.  Take away your pride.”  And that worked.

“And of course, many years have passed, and that helped too.”  He continued.

“Wow, that must be very difficult.” I told him, “I do not want to say I can only imagine.  Since many people told me that, and I always replied it’s worse than you can imagine.”

He laughed and agreed, “Only when you were in that situation, then you may be able to feel...”

 

At this Thanksgiving day, I thank the Lord that He loves all His sheep.  He would find the lost sheep no matter how long it takes.
