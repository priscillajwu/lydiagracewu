July 25, 2006, Tuesday

It’s now the third week of Lydia’s second chemotherapy. It seems now that Lydia’s second chemotherapy was worse than her first chemotherapy (although the first week of her second chemo, we felt that the second time was better than the first time).

This exactly showed that we could do very little. We thought we could do better. No, not really, only if we can totally trust in the Lord. Then, better or worse, God is always watching us.

Lydia’s conditions could change several times a day. Recent days, we do not go by days, but go by hours.

To my surprise, Lydia still wanted to go the summer camp held by the Children’s hospital. Somehow she is feeling comfortable there.

She threw up during the ride to the camp, feeling pretty sick while she was there, and she had tough time during the night after she came home.

By the time she came home from the camp in the afternoon, all her siblings were heading out for the swim meet and party afterwards. Lydia had to watch her sisters go out having fun while she was in severe suffering.

Lydia was in deep sorrow tonight, home alone with mom. That troubled me a lot. I could not limit the activities of her siblings. That is not right. However, keep her siblings at home usually help Lydia to cope with her suffering.

I pray to God, please give me wisdom of what to do in a daily bases…
