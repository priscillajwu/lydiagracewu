November 21, 2006, Tuesday

Today, three months after Lydia passed away.

 

I took off from work at noon to attend Priscilla’s school Thanksgiving feast.  I met many Lydia’s previous teachers and staffs.  One teacher showed us Lydia’s work which was being demonstrated to the current students.  Looking at Lydia’s handwriting, I had hard time to hold my tears.  By the time I left school, I was in sorrow again.  I must be the weakest dad in the world according to hold tears in my eyes.

 

Mom prepared a couple dozens of solar lights.  We went to Lydia’s gravesite in the late afternoon.  All the girls worked very hard to put the lights together and put around Lydia’s grave.  It was cold, but we enjoyed the time decorating Lydia’s grave.  We also decorated the little tree behind it.  All the red leaves were gone, but we wrapped around with the red and silver tinsels.  So that friends can have easier time to locate the Lydia’s gravesite.

By the time we finished, it’s dark already.  The solar lights are supposed to absorb the solar energy, and it will light up at night.

Lydia was a little light to shine.  Mom got this great idea to put these solar lights there.

We should all be the light to shine with the strength and energy from the Lord.  Just like Lydia…

 

We need to embrace entire family very tightly in these holiday seasons.  And we are sure the Lord will help and comfort us with His grace and mercy.
