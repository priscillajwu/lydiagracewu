October 21, 2006, Saturday

Two months anniversary.

Mom had very tough time today.  She was not able to sleep last night, and cried a lot, really a lot, all the way, until this afternoon.

 

In the afternoon, we have guests arrived, exchange students from England to live with us.  It’s wonderful we can open our home and host two foreign students.  If Lydia still doing chemotherapy as originally planned, with very low immune system, we would not be able to be the host family.

All three girls were really excited, they helped mom to get everything ready for the guests.  So, mom got a lot of help to put away some of Lydia’s stuff from the family room.  Otherwise, mom would never be able to do it.  She would weep and cry and grieve, and still not able to finish it.

 

At night, we went to the music hall for the Cincinnati Symphony concert, one of the favorite activities of the family.

This is the first time without Lydia, but with two guests from England.

Mom shared the story about Lydia.  A few years ago, Lydia was much younger, she saw the candy booth in the lobby of the music hall.  There were many candies and chocolate fudge for sale.  I, a stingy father, never bought those for the girls.  So, Lydia went by herself to ask how much is the candy.  (It was a lot of money for a little piece.)  The lady who was selling the fudge, looking at Lydia being such a cute girl, gave Lydia a piece for free.  Lydia came back with a piece of fudge on her hand with innocent look.  Everybody laughed, Lydia shared with Priscilla.

While mom shared the story, I cried inside.  I missed Lydia so much.

 

On the way to the parking lot, Priscilla was not willing to walk since she got wound in her feet (she wore Lydia’s shoes, and did not fit well).  So, I piggybacked her.  I walked and all of sudden, the sorrow attacked…

Piggybacking Deborah when she was younger, or Priscilla, like tonight, have been a very normal thing for me.  But Lydia never needed that.  Lydia, since she started walking (really early, probably before one year old), she always wanted to walk by herself, seldom asking mom or dad to hold her.  She would walk and hold mommy or daddy’s hand, instead of being held.

Mom and daddy were always amazed about Lydia that she was willing to walk by herself, without whining or complaining.  In the family vacation, hiking is always an important part.  Lydia was the one keep walking.  Whenever I suggested some more adventures, she would be the first one say good, let’s go.  Very different from the other three girls.

However, when Lydia was sick, I did piggyback her a few times.  She was weak physically…

 

Sorrow attacks, heart pains, often came from these memories.  Can I not think about it?  No way. 

Sorrow and suffering continue everyday.  Wow, God is really giving me hard lessons.
