December 23, 2006, Saturday

It’s over four months after Lydia passed away.

There were not any ten minutes gone by that I did not think of Lydia.

However, God is still faithful and so good.  We are experiencing JOY and PEACE at this holiday season.  Knowing we are in prayers of many brought us comfort.

We received a lot of emails, gifts cards and greetings from all over the world.  Thank you all so much.  God is really watching us and carrying us through this difficult time.  We are truly experiencing it.

 

Last three days, we did three activities we never did before and but talked about doing it for years, NutCracker ballet, Kentucky live horse racing, and Broadway show.  Yes, it was interesting and of enough fun.  But there was something missing.  I was not particular excited or enjoyed, before, during, or after the activities.

I wondered why was that.  Before, with four kids, we had a lot easier time to make up mind of things to do.  But now we have three kids, we have more difficulty to get agreement among the families.  We could not decide the vacation plan for the Christmas break, and end up staying home.  With some discussion, there were a few possibilities.

“Daddy has changed after Lydia died.”  One said.  I could not deny that being a part of it.

“We are all grieving.  That de-motivated us.”  Another said.  That is very true.

But I think the major part is that the most motivated and passionate member of the family is no longer here.  Lydia is always interested in trying new things, new food, going new places.  I can still recall her saying,

“Yeah, let’s go.”

“Let’s do it.” 

With her quick wit and the ability to convince and energize (with her zest), the rest of the family often went along with her.

It’s amazing to find out that, a middle child, and being so young, can have so much influence to the entire family.

We have hard time adjusting ourselves to live the life without Lydia.

 

We watched a Christian movie yesterday.  It’s very inspiring.  The main character has suffered tremendous losses in her life, one after another.  She grew up as the only child, her mother died when she was small, father died after her graduate from college without much asset left to her, fiance died in the war, the only son (and daughter in law) died in the car accident, after raising up the only grand daughter the husband had Alzheimer, and grand daughter lived an immoral life.  It was extremely difficult.  She asked millions times of WHY.  No answer from God.  What kept her move on is her FAITH.

“I have decided to follow the Lord, no turning back, no turning back…” was the main theme in the movie.

I thank God for the reminder.
