November 11, 2006, Saturday

A close friend gave us a garden stone, with the words,

“IF TEARS COULD BUILD A STARWAY,

AND MEMORIES A LANE,

I’D WALK RIGHT UP TO HEAVEN

AND BRING YOU HOME AGAIN.”

This garden stone made us cry.

“I just wish I could have her back.” Mom said.

 

 

Several dear family friends from our church got together tonight.  We had great dinner together.  Then we sat together and had very good sharing.  Mom and dad were grateful and comforted that many brothers and sisters really care about us.  They really do.  Lydia’s death was a huge loss and emotional struggle for them and their children.  We shed a lot of tears.  A few times, it’s hard for us to bear while listening others sharing about how they miss Lydia. 

We shared and prayed together.  I believe God has comforted each of us.  My stomach may continue aching, mom’s eyes may continue hurting.  But God’s grace is with us all the time.

 

Often, we pray for the health, joy, and peace for our family.  However, how can we appreciate true peace if we never experienced ultimate suffering, pain and fear.  A true joy does not come from an easy life, but from trusting God even in the most difficult situation.

Of course, we could decide not to grieve, not to think about it, pretend it won’t affect me, and even deny it.  But is that God wanting us to do?  For people who don’t believe in God, this is the best way out.  But in that case, then value of suffering is just matter of opinion or choice of attitude.  Life becomes so shallow.  How can we experience God, the much greater good…
