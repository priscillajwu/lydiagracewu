September 11, 2006, Monday

911.  This is a sad day for many.

For whatever reason, I felt extremely sad today.  I went to Lydia’s grave and cried terribly.  Definitely one of the worst since Lydia passed away.

 

 

“Why are you three sisters doing so well?  How did you do it?  Don’t you miss Lydia?  ”  Mom asked Esther.

“Of course I miss her, I miss her all the time.”  Esther answered, “But we should be happy for her.  She has done a good job in her life.  Not like us, we still got a lot of things to do, to work hard, and to please God.  You and daddy are too focus on YOUR loss of Lydia.”

 

Esther was right.  And we knew that is part of our problem too.  We thank God for the maturity of Esther, only fifteen years old.  She helped mom and dad greatly in many ways.  She often said she could identify Lydia with herself the most.  (This made me sad also.)

Faith of the children is amazing.  We are glad that we have raised them with the faith in the Christ Jesus.

 

What I could not understand is that why the sorrow of loss of Lydia is so great that continued hurting us so badly. 

Maybe it’s the cup we have to take.  (Matthew 26:42)  It’s not going to be easy for us.
