March 14, 06

My dear prayer heroes,

 

Lydia went to University Hospital Barrett cancer center for X-ray.  She has been a very good patient and very cooperated.  Everybody likes her in the cancer center, just like in Children Hospital.  (I don’t know if I could be that likable if I were sick.)  The technicians took several X-ray films in order to position her during the radiation treatment.  They put quite a few tapes and marks on her body, in a lot of places.  We were told it’s very important to keep them on for the entire course of the radiation.

In the evening, after mom helped Lydia bathing, (so not to mess up with those marks and tapes) while mom drained the bath tub and picked up the clothes for her, she peeked Lydia and saw she was quietly checking those tapes and marks, one by one, touched them, counted them, and saw them from the mirror, by twisting her body around….

Mom cried when she saw what Lydia was doing.  I was not there, but I can imagine a small body with many marks and tapes, some small, some bigger, some visible, some might be covered by the clothes.  It was not beautiful.  Then, mom pictured later when Lydia’s hair starts to fall.  Mom just could not stand the feeling about that...  So, mom wanted to get hair wig for Lydia.  Well, I have different opinion about it (or maybe I was just not ready for her possible hair loss side effect yet.)  I would like to wait and see if we really need one.  Not because it costs so much, I think, maybe Lydia would not lose hair.  I suggested we wait and see.  (But deep in Lydia’s mind, she may like to have one, just dare not to ask for it...)

Well, tonight, Lydia got a gift, guess what, a black color hair wig, (which could be very difficult to get here in US.)  A sister from our church just came back from Shanghai brought her one.  Lydia was so happy and very interested in it.  She tried it on with a lot of fun.  Thank the Lord for His provision.

Mom bought several hats for Lydia today.  Lydia was again very happy to try those hats tonight.

Also, today, Lydia got a Play Station gift from another aunt.  It can hook up to a electric floor mat and she can do exercises.  That will be good for her physical therapy.  So, she played and jumped back and forth, left and right, on the mat (jumping? just two weeks after her second brain surgery?  I am not kidding.)

I am sure Lydia is ready for the radiation, which starts tomorrow.  Again, we hope the side effects can be minimal.  She has been doing so well so far.  Praise the Lord.  I believe she will continue doing well.

Thank you for your prayers, love, care, and supports.  We are so blessed.  Oh Lord, thank You.

(2 Corinthians 1:11)

 

In Him,

Simon
