October 19, 2006, Thursday

Mom started to distribute some wristbands at several public places where people can get it.

One of Priscilla’s classmates went to orthodontics appointment today and came back with the wristband on his hand.  The entire class was amazed about it.  Everybody wanted one.  It’s really distinguishing and looked really special.

Since Lydia’s dream was to become a missionary.  Now, she can no longer be the missionary herself on earth.  However, we can all be the missionaries for her to tell her story how she loved the Lord and how her faith helped her to fight a good fight, and keep the never-failing hope till the last minute of her life.

Another character Lydia has was that she worked very hard for the school.  Although being so ill, she would take whatever opportunity to finish her school assignment, to participate musical play, spelling bee, and graduated from the elementary school between radiation therapy and chemotherapy.  She was never a whining patient, but a very strong and determined hero to many, children and adults.

Because of her love for the Lord, her love of learning, sports, music, and her love of life, any proceeds from people’s donation through distributing the wristband, we would like to support missionary and funding Lydia memorial scholarship which promotes Lydia’s spirit of learning, hard work, and perseverance.

By wearing the wristband, we may be able to start the topic to share about Lydia’s story, and her faith, then share God’s love, and salvation.

 

If there is no God, Lydia’s passing would not make any sense.  Her life, including our lives would not make any sense either.  But because of our faithful God, I have ultimate comfort and hope about Lydia and my final destiny. 

 

God gave me Lydia for only ten years (I wept terribly now), and He has taken away from me.  Instead of asking thousand times of “why”, now, although still suffering from the sorrow of loss, it’s my mission and burden to tell Lydia’s story and continued my job God gave me on earth, to live for God, and to honor God like Lydia, for she has done her part.

The greatest comfort we have is hearing someone was saved, inspired, or revived, because the testimony of Lydia.  And so far God is generous about giving us the comforts…
