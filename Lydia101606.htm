October 16, 2006, Monday

Priscilla finished her homework really early today, hoping she could have some fun time. 

“Can you play with me?” Priscilla asked around.

Nobody said no, but nobody could play with her.

Two older sisters really had too much work to do at night.  Mommy was helping one of them.  And I had tons of emails to go through.  Knowing Priscilla has no one to play with made me feel so bad and sad.  I got up right away and went to basement with Priscilla to play some games with her, with very sorrowful heart.

I was not really good at those games.  Esther and Deborah were probably too good.  Lydia would be the best one to play for Priscilla.

“You can invite friends over to play.” I told Priscilla.  (For many years, they seldom needed friends over.  Four girls paired up and occupied each other nicely.)

“Right now?”  Priscilla was not sure.

“No, it’s too late.  I mean next time, we can arrange something...”

Deborah was nice enough to sleep with Priscilla once a while.  Mom too.  I did it several times only during the weekend.  Most of time, Priscilla sleeps by herself in the room with a lot of Lydia’s stuff in there.  Priscilla is doing quite well, because I won’t be able to handle that myself.

After Priscilla went to bed, Esther and Deborah mentioned to mom and dad that they missed Lydia a lot.  Only that they do not cry in front of us or other people, but in their own bed…

 

We tried to watch the video taping of Lydia’s farewell celebration party for the very first time.  It was impossible.  We got tearful right away.  We could only fast forward most of them.  Mom could not watch it at all.

 

“Is it ever be possible that we can feel happy when we think of Lydia?”  I asked mom.

“I am trying.” Mom said.

A lot of times, I think mom is tougher than me.

 

We received a prayer today shared by a sister in Christ,

"Dear God, I have never thanked You for my thorns. I have thanked You a thousand times for my roses, but never once for my thorns. Teach me the glory of the cross I bear; teach me the value of my thorns. Show me that, through my tears, the colors of Your rainbow look much more brilliant. Show me that I have climbed closer to You along the path of pain."
