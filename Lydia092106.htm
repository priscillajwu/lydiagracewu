September 21, 2006, Thursday

Lydia may be celebrating her one month anniversary in the paradise.

Now, one month later, I still miss Lydia terribly.  Everyday is a difficult day.  I have lost some weight.  However, my steps are heavier than before.  Surviving seems a little fake nowadays.

Different from before, I spent a lot more time with three girls.  Before Lydia got ill, I did not spend enough time with any of them.  After Lydia got sick, I spent as much time as I could to be with Lydia.  Now, I would hold three girls, look at them, and smile at them.  What can be more precious?

Also, I sigh a lot.  I shook head without knowing it.  Several times a day, I would feel my heart got pierced.  Eyes filled with tears becomes a regular routine…

In a way, from the appearance, everything has gone back to normal.  (How could it be normal? How can it be normal without Lydia?)  Three girls are doing fine as far as everybody can see.  Grandma is doing fine.  Mom and dad?  Severely troubled and hurt.  We were often frightened with dreams, and terrified by what had happened to us.

 

We know and believe that God does not make mistake (Romans 8:28).  However, it is very hard for us to go on.  Without God’s mercy and prayers of many, it would be impossible to get through everyday.

 

Mom is thinking very hard about what to do next.  I have a lot of discussion with her.  Many possibilities.  Our main purpose is to share Lydia’s life for the Kingdom of God. 

Lydia has lived an amazing life.  She is truly a gift from the Lord.  Not only an inspiration, but also a role model, a living witness, for many of us, children or adults, in a lot of ways.

 

Lydia has a very tender heart.  This has been shared to us again and again by many people (teachers, parents, classmates, friends) who knew her, since the evening of August 21, when rememberinglydia@zoomtown.com email started.  Of course, that made us cry, but we were greatly comforted.

 

Although Lydia is gone, we continue praying that God can use her life as a blessing to many people around the globe.
