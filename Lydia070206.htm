July 2, 2006, Sunday

To cheer up Lydia, I tried to come up with something fun. It has to be something special, and we could not go far away (the doctors and nurses have been reminded us again and again, do not go out of town or too far away, just in case Lydia needs any emergency care).

Lydia and Priscilla wanted to watch movie. I was not sure that was a good idea, since there would be a lot of people in the theater and it’s indoors. Then, I decided to go to the Winton Woods which has a boat house. We could have some fun renting the boat.

It was terrible. I deeply regretted the idea.

When we got there, I wanted to get something easy like motor boat. However, Lydia wanted the pedal boat, so she can pedal. For motor boat, she could only sit there.

The weather was very hot, up in 90’s. There was no shade in the pedal boat. Priscilla soon started whining, and made everybody in the boat miserable. We were in the middle of the lake, and had trouble to maneuver the boat. Lydia tried very hard to pedal (she had hat and long sleeve clothes and pants in such a hot weather), but Priscilla just would not cooperate, and whine all the way. It was really too hot.

So, we had to rush home shortly. I was really angry with Priscilla, and that made her crying and felt really sorry…

What happened later was that Lydia helped Priscilla feel better by sitting next to her and held Priscilla in her arms. She said it was all her fault. That calmed me down all of sudden. I felt extremely bad. I had to pray, and realized my weakness. I had to apologize.

Something to make up in the evening. We bought a small air inflated water pool last week. Since it’s so hot, I made it to work in the back yard. Lydia, Priscilla, and I were in the water having a lot of fun. They have forgotten what happened earlier today.

I found a long piece of hair in the water, "Whose hair?" I asked while brought the hair out of the water.

"It can’t be mine, since now I do not have hair." Lydia laughed.

"Not mine, I don’t have hair either." I said.

"OK, OK, it’s mine." Priscilla said and also laughed.

Although we were joking around, hearing Lydia said she does not have hair, still got me a bit of tears in my eyes…

Well, thank God for the day. A lot of lessons to learn…
