My dear prayer heroes,

Now, Lydia has another tumor, the location is inoperable according to the neurosurgeon. So, no surgery is planned. The radiation dose will be increased and lengthened. The side effects and permanent damages could be exponentially more.

Although situation is very tough, God has told us not to be scared. (Joshua 1:9)

Please continue praying for Lydia. Apparently, God has allowed all of these to happen. May God’s name be glorified. He has the healing power. He is in control of everything, and He is our God, our Lord, and He has mercy and grace. I have no reason not to believe God is going to help us, He is going to heal Lydia, and He is going to use Lydia for the great testimony of His wondrous acts. Most importantly, lift up our faith. He is our true father, nothing under the sun can be relied on or depended on, only our Lord, Christ Jesus.

Lydia continued having severe pain. The steroid and pain medicines are closely taken and controlled. The IV liquid made Lydia needed to go to restroom every one to two hours. When I watched Lydia overnight at hospital. I could get up and help her to restroom, then I came back, and I could fall asleep within minutes. Mom could not. So, she has trained Lydia to go to restroom by herself. Lydia would get up in the middle of night, by herself, unplug the IV, and pushed the IV (which is a lot taller than her) and slowly walked to the restroom, urinated, washed hand, and then slowly walked back, plugged the IV, climbed back to her bed, and tried to go back to sleep.

I saw it tonight, a small body, pushed the IV, and slowly (making sure the tubes are not tangled) walked to the restroom…., nicely and quietly.  It’s very cute  It also made me cry…. Truly, it’s hard to watch. Either I jumped to help her, or just don’t look….

Thank the Lord and praise the Lord that Lydia is a strong girl. Let’s continue praying for her. Let’s have faith in our Lord, and thank you very much for keeping her in your prayers.

In Him,
