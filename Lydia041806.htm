April 18, 2006, Tuesday

This is one of those emotionally very difficult nights.

Lydia hid herself in the corner of living room and cried quietly, while nobody noticed for a while. Mom found her, and asked her what’s going on.

She would not answer, I had to pick her up and found out she was crying badly. Then, she started talking with a lot of tears. We were worried since her pulse was so fast, and blood pressure was very low nowadays.

"I felt I am so useless. I used to be able to cover others, help others. Now I need to be taken care…."

"I had the nightmare about getting the cancer before, I never thought I would really get it."

"I am so scared…."

"If the cancer recur, will I need to go through radiation and chemo therapy again?"

"Can I ever swim again when summer comes?

"I am scared I can no longer have normal life again."

"When can we take a trip… can I do it?"

She said so much, with non-stopping crying. Her skin around the eyes got so red (her skin is very sensitive now).

It was very difficult for all of us. Mom, Dad, and Esther tried to comfort her. Deborah and Priscilla kept quiet nearby.

Emotional pain and suffering sometimes were worse than physical. With hope in God is the only thing which can comfort us.

After probably an hour, Lydia finally felt better and said, "God has all of these planned years ago, right?"

"Actually He knew it before you were even born." Mom said.

Lydia finally smiled. It’s a great comfort knowing that our heavenly father is looking after us very closely…
