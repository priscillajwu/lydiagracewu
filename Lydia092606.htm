September 26, 2006, Tuesday

An excerpt of an email from a young lady exactly one week after Lydia’s farewell celebration party.  We received it again today sent by her father…

 

I wrote this….not knowing what would come out. I wouldn't even say it is a memory, but it is a reminder of how she touched our lives…even away from Cincinnati. For those in college, those in Cleveland, I even had friends in Michigan who knew about Lydia and were praying for her. That's how much she was loved and how much she had touched us. How much we felt the loss as well. So for her story to be told, I wrote this.

its been a week exactly.
its hard to gather all of your thoughts after a time like that...
i've been sitting here typing and deleting, typing and deleting...
the email that was written by the boy that was read--i feel just like him...
i feel like i've lost a friend.

i was off in college when i first heard about lydia. i didn't know her that well but i remember always seeing the sisters traveling in packs of 4, always helping each other out, following esther. They were always on their best behavior wherever they went. I remember going to the Mason church to talk to the parents and afterwards they wanted us to spend some time with the children. Being the typical little kids, they had a short attention span and i'm pretty sure they weren't interested in what we had to say. but as i looked out in the crowd, i remember seeing lydia just sitting there and trying to tell the girls that she was sitting with to quiet down and listen to what we had to say. i don't remember how old she was, but she just had this aura about her cause people listened to her.

All i had to know what was going on were the updates. and i felt like i was going on the journey with the family. My heart went out to the family, to the girls. I remember when i was in cleveland for piano camp one summer and they were living there, they kept an eye out for me, made sure everything was alright. They are always looking out for people. I think that's where lydia got her caring heart for others. So to hear the stories of how she kept giving back to others even in her time of sickness only gave us a bigger picture of how big her heart was.

When i went to the hospital after getting back from taiwan-- she was still fighting for life, talking to us, keeping the focus off of her. She couldn't see us that well but she still asked who we were and pointed us all out. "i love you, thank you" those were the words that came out of her mouth. Even to us, she said it...and i didn't even know her that well. a heart of a warrior.

what got me the most was how many people had showed up for her celebration to see how many people she had touched with the joys of her life. through singing, playing the piano, the cello, even school, she did it with a smile.
the displays were beautiful, the words from friends at church and school to lydia hit a chord. people came from Cleveland, from her neighborhood, everywhere near and far. And for those who have read about her or even heard about her, for the continued prayers, they were there that day. For that afternoon, we were all a family. We were all missing Lydia.

Thank you for sharing her with us. Seeing the slideshow, singing her favorite song, at the end, I realized, she DID have a good life. She did her deed. just how we all came together to talk about her life, her triumphs, her victories. she touched our hearts, she planted her seed in us. we were lucky to know her while she was still here. she lives in us and her story WILL live on. She is at a better place. My friend is looking down on us.

the heart of a warrior with a child-like faith. thank you.

……

 

Yes, Lydia was born in Cleveland.  We moved back to Cincinnati when she was four years old.

 

Nowadays, at dinner table, grandma would sit on the chair I used to sit.  I have been using Lydia’s chair.  So, it’s still six people on the table.  Later, after grandma leaves, I probably would continue sit on Lydia’s chair which has been next to mommy ever since Lydia was born…

Tonight, after dinner, everybody finished and left, except mom and dad.  We talked, and looked at each other, then we both burst into tears.  One of the girls came to find out what happened.  Mom went to wash dishes with water flowing.  I escaped into bathroom, crying badly inside, without making sound.  I looked into the mirror of myself.  My hair slowly grew back.  Recall the night I shave the head with Lydia…  My heart not just aches, but piercingly, sharply painful…  I had hard time to stand straight.

 

It just came to me that many read this first thing in the morning.  Sorry, I have no intention to make you sad.  I would like to thank God for your love and prayer.

May God bless you.  We love you.

 

We are thinking to make some bracelets about Lydia.  When we wear it we can share Lydia’s story to people around us.  Through sharing Lydia’s life, her faith, love, and hope, we pray that many can be saved for the kingdom of God.

May God’s name be glorified.
