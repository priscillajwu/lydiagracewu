October 3, 2006, Tuesday

The email we received today from Lydia’s piano teacher,

“……

Life continues to bring pain and sadness, but yet we need to praise God because He brings comfort and peace to  us all when we face these difficulties.  We can only come to him for comfort because He is our Heavenly Father with everlasting love.  Over the weekend while we were in New York City, we received news that Benjamin's school principal, passed away suddenly. He was young, strong, handsome, with the kindest heart.   He was a dear dear friend to us.  We loved him and his wife dearly and had developed a very special friendship over the past seven years.  He was so looking forward for Benjamin to return from his New York concert and was planning on celebrating with him.  He suddenly fell ill last Friday, was sent to the hospital early Saturday morning, and went to be with the Lord on Sunday morning.  He and his wife have dedicated their entire lives to serving God.  They, like your family, have truly lived a Christ-like life.  LBA is not just a school.  It is a mission to bring young children up in the way God wants us to so that they will not go astray.  The principal stood at the front door every morning and afternoon to greet the children and see them off in the afternoon.  He is now gone. The image of him not being there is unbearable.   The first thing that came to my mind was, "He must be playing with Lydia now."  When he passed away, his wife said, "He is happy playing with the children in heaven now." 

It has been so difficult.  Please pray for his wife and his parents.  He and his wife did not have any children.  He loved all his students like his own children.  We have truly experienced his love for us and Benjamin.  It will be a very long and lonely road for his wife.  She just recovered from her chemotherapy from cancer a few months ago and has now lost her husband.

We continue to pray for you and the principal’s family.  We loved him just like we loved Lydia.  May God grant us all peace and hope as we all continue to battle with the pain...

……”

 

I have quite a few Sunday school students attended LBA.  I led the entire Sunday school students praying for the principal this past Sunday morning.  I was so sad when I read this email.  My heart broke for them.  I was very vulnerable for this, for it put me in terrible grieving instantly.

 

 

Tonight, I talked to a neighbor, who loves the Lord sincerely.

“Being where Lydia is now, although she loves and misses the family so much, I am sure she does not want to come back.”  He said.

“Hey, that’s a good one.  Thank you, brother.”  It lighted me up.

It would be so nice when it’s my turn to get there, the place which is the most magnificent.
