January 21, 2007, Sunday

It’s five months today since Lydia left us.

It’s also the first winter snow with a lot of accumulation after Lydia passed away.

Priscilla would like to play with the snow since early morning.  She has been asking around.  Nobody was interested.  Usually, Lydia and Priscilla would go out together in no time.

So, Priscilla went out to the yard by herself.  Mom and I looked through the window with a lot of sadness.  Life has been very different for Priscilla.

After a while, Priscilla got bored and came in to ask again if anyone would go out with her.

Slowly, I put on my snow boots and got out.  Not to play with Priscilla but just to keep her company, I was working on shoveling the driveway.

Of course, Priscilla would want to make a snowman.  But it’s too much a task for her.

I don’t know when Esther and Deborah came out to help Priscilla.  It was nice.  But looking at three girls rolling the snowball is still very awkward.  It should have been four girls.  It felt so strange and incomplete.

 

Later, I overcame my sorrow and got in to help rolling the snowball.   At the end, we built a big snowman with eyes, nose, and mouth from Priscilla’s ideas.  She wanted to build a teddy bear.  I believe Priscilla was pretty happy.  If Lydia were around, we would have built it with a lot more fun, quicker and better decoration.  Because she would energize, motivate and give a lot of suggestions and comments…

 

Life has been very different without Lydia.  I have difficulty to adjust.  I missed her so much (days and nights, a lot of dreams lately).  But the world has been going on without any sign of slowing down.  So often, I have to seek the comfort from the Lord only (nobody else can provide), in many sleepless dark nights.
