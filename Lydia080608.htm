August 6, 2008, Wednesday

It’s Lydia’s birthday.

It’s a lot more difficult than I would like to be.

But thank the Lord for He is full of grace and mercy.

Started a few days ago, we have received quite a few cards and emails making sure we know that we are in their thoughts and prayers.  That gave us a great comfort.

It’s amazing.  It has been two years.  And God still work through many amazing ways to help us.  I thought people would have forgotten by now or got impatient about our continuous grieving.  And especially I have not been sharing much lately.  Many people asked us how are we doing since they have not seen much from the web site.

Well, thanks for asking.  Thank the Lord, and praise the Lord, we are still surviving.

 

Still, during the month of August, it’s very difficult.

I do not like August.  Lydia was born and died in August.    My father and brother died in August.  I was born in August…

When living a life full of sorrow, you don’t feel celebrating the birthday at all.  (Job 3:1-13)

Of course, that is only my feeling.  God would know the best for me and for all of us.  Who am I to argue with God?

 

Lydia’s book in Chinese is going to be published soon.  That is probably a good earthly birthday gift to Lydia.

We are still not able to read any of previous entries in this web site.  It’s too difficult for us.  But because of the need to clarify a few questions from the editor of the publisher, I quickly scanned and read a few entries.  No doubt, way too difficult for me.

But you know what?  I, myself, was inspired and encouraged by Lydia’s story and our own testimony.  Isn’t that amazing?

 

We pray that God would use Lydia’s life for His Kingdom.  And that would bring a lot of joy over sorrow.
