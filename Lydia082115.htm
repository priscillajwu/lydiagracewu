August 21, 2015, Friday

Our Star

 

 

 

Lying on my back

 

Soft feathers pillow my head

 

My limbs cocooned in a warm blanket

 

My body in perfect contentment

 

 

 

But I stare into the darkness

 

A black so deep

 

It seems to suck me in

 

And consume me

 

 

 

But in the utter darkness

 

There is a faint glow

 

A tiny star

 

I can just barely discern it

 

 

 

It breaks the unending night

 

This faint glow

 

It prevents me from disappearing

 

It keeps me

 

Grounded on this earth

 

 

 

*

 

 

 

I remember when they first came

 

Twenty of them,

 

Packed into a box

 

Some could cover my palm

 

And others just fit my thumb

 

 

 

We hung them up with string and sticky tack

 

Leaning from the top of the bunk bed

 

We relished the thrill of nearly falling

 

Laughing, we stepped back

 

And admired our handiwork

 

 

 

Twenty stars,

 

Scattered the ceiling

 

Some low, some high

 

Some tiny, some large

 

But we loved it

 

 

 

When we went to sleep

 

In the darkness we would whisper and giggle

 

And stare at the lights that glittered our sky

 

Oh how I wish I could be there again.

 

 

 

Because suddenly,

 

I was staring at the stars alone.

 

I could no longer laugh.

 

The stars that once brought so much wonder and joy

 

Now brought pain and emptiness.

 

 

 

 

 

As the years passed,

 

The stars began to fall.

 

One by one, the sky became darker.

 

The stars disappeared

 

And so did our memories.

 

 

 

I left that room,

 

Did you know?

 

I could no longer bear to see the remaining stars

 

As they disappeared,

 

So did you.

 

From memories, conversations, pictures.

 

 

 

I thought it was better that way.

 

Without memories,

 

There was no pain.

 

Just emptiness.

 

 

 

*

 

 

 

It’s been nine years.

 

Can you believe it?

 

The room we once shared

 

Has changed so much

 

I doubt you would recognize it.

 

 

 

One thing hasn’t changed though.

 

The star.

 

Just one.

 

Over the years, just one stayed.

 

Just one kept holding on.

 

 

 

When I see its glow,

 

I no longer try to block the pain

 

But embrace the bittersweet memories

 

Of the joy we shared.

 

 

 

When its faint glow fades

 

As the hours of the night pass,

 

I stare into the utter darkness.

 

I may not be able to see it anymore

 

But I know it’s there.

 

 

 

Just like you.
