March 8, 06

A conversation between Esther and Lydia.

"The most fear people have for brain surgery is that they may die in the operation, never wake up again." Esther said.

"No, that is not scary, if I do not wake up, then I will be with the Lord Jesus." Lydia said, "what I feared the most is that I woke up in the middle of surgery, and found out my head is open. That will be pretty scary." Isn’t that something! About the Children’s faith.

Since grandma is leaving tomorrow. Everybody would like to say something to thank grandma, while daddy was video taping it. Three sisters said something about thanking her for fixing them the meals and daily schedule. Lydia said, "Thank you for taking care of sisters, so that Mom and Dad can take care of me in hospital." One after another, everyone said something to appreciate, including mom and dad. Grandma was very happy. We were all happy and thankful.

Yes, thank the Lord for the grandma to be able to be with us in this greatest trial ever in our family. As matter of fact, this is the longest time grandma ever spent with us. It was not easy for her, since she really not used many things here (she lived in Taiwan for all her life.). During last month, she clogged kitchen sink, broke the vacuum machine…. Because she was really trying very hard to help us.

I always remember the moment I picked her up in the airport, the day after Lydia was diagnosed with brain tumor. She said,

"Not to worry, everything will be OK, I have prayed. I have faith that almighty God will watch over us…."

It’s a great blessing that we have a grandma with great faith. Thank the Lord.

In Him,

Simon
