September 25, 2006, Monday

One neighbor shared with me today that he noticed my daughters did attend the first week of school as usual, which was the week Lydia passed away and that Saturday was Lydia’s farewell celebration party, as well as that I am back to work, and back to serve in the church right away.  All of these amazed him.

It’s the first person to share with me like that.  I never thought about it that way.  I thought we are grieving terribly, and we are mal-functioned in many ways.  However, he saw it differently.

I can only thank God for He deserves all the glory.  And it’s because of Him that the prayers of many friends and brothers and sisters did lift us up. 

 

Today, I posted on the wall of my office one of my favorite picture of Lydia which was taken in December 26, 2005 in Tampa, Florida during the family vacation, roughly one month before she developed headache.  I enlarged it to a pretty good size.  I was able to look at it and not crying crazily.  Thank God, I would not be able to do that just last week.

During Lydia’s illness, she often looked at the pictures we took in late December 2005 vacation to Disney world and beach in Florida.  She would say that, “I was still fine back then.” Then she would be silent for a while.  It was heart breaking to hear her say that.

How precious.  When I took the picture, did I know I would lose her eight months later?  Of course not.  Well, we still thank God, our creator and savior.  As of today, I have hope because of Christ Jesus.  We have 100% of hope with the Lord and Lydia about the future.

 

In the evening, I went to pick up Deborah from a community orchestra practice.  She was pretty happy.  This is her week.  She got baptized yesterday.  She was elected the student of the week of the junior high school, she won the tennis match today competing with other junior high schooler, and she will have another singing and dancing practice tomorrow…

“Do you enjoy all of these?” I asked Deborah.

“Yeah.”

“I am sure Lydia would enjoy all of these too.  Why didn’t she be given the chance?”  I asked.  (I was pretty sure Lydia would be playing cello if she is still around while Deborah is the violinist in the orchestra.)

“Well, a lot of times, I got overwhelmed.”  Deborah continued, “Maybe, she is doing all these fun things up there too.  She may be flying, and that is cool.”

“Well said.”  I smiled.

While we continue guessing what Lydia is doing now.  We are sure she is doing quite good now.

 

Priscilla got into trouble tonight again (a typical youngest child behavior?).  And mom and dad and especially Priscilla were very upset.  Mom and dad were waiting for her explanation what’s going on.

“I really want to change.”  Priscilla said, “When Lydia was alive, she would stand up for me.  I guess I need to do it myself now.”

“Yes, you are right.”  I said. “Now , tell me what happened….”

“Is that why she died so that I can change?” said Priscilla.

Hearing what she said, I was in extreme sorrow.  Oh Lord, we do not have the answer for a lot of things.  But, please give us strength to go through everyday.

At the end, Priscilla asked me, “Would you forgive me?”

“Of course, I will always forgive you.”

“I love you, daddy.”

“I love you too, the same as Lydia, the same as Esther, and Deborah.”  I said.

“I thank God for our family.”  Priscilla said

“Yes, me too, even though we lost Lydia…”  I almost cried, “Go to bed, good night.”

I cried badly after she went to bed.  It’s another day….
