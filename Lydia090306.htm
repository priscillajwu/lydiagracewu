September 3, 2006, Sunday

For many nights, the quality of sleep has been poor.  Both mom and dad would wake up many times a night.  Probably we still continued the sleeping pattern like the last several months.  However, before, we woke up to take care of Lydia, with nice things to do with Lydia, to massage her, to help her feel better.  Now, we woke up with heavy hearts, sweats all over, and feeling of severe loss.  Mom display Lydia’s picture in front of us in our bedroom (Some may say it’s not a good idea, but mom wants it).  We would look at her and talk and weep, and talk more, weep more.  Don’t we know she is with the Lord and must be really good?  Yes, we do.  We just miss her too much, and not used to the life without her.  For ten years, we have her in our life everyday.  Now, it’s very different.  It’s very hard to get used to it.

Mom and girls are attending English worship and Chinese worship.  Running between two churches (mother church and branch church are half an hour apart) on Sunday did take some extra efforts.  Mom is doing pretty good when with brothers and sisters.  She already got her hands back in to several ministries, like children, and youth programs.  I am amazed how she could do it.  God really gave mom tough characters.  She takes care of family, and keeps on top of everything of three girls.

As for me, I am not sure I am doing fine.  A neighbor came to me and said it must be tough because it feels like a hole in my heart.  I told her, not only a hole, I feel my entire life has been taken away from me.  I am severely crippled.  I need to remind myself and take extra strength to keep up with things around me.  Some said, mom and dad would grieve differently.  Well, I am surprisingly weaker than I thought.  My sorrow was way deeper than I could ever imagine.  I prayed to God to help ease my pain and sorrow, so I could be strong and showed God’s help on me.  Maybe that is not God’s way.  (2 Corinthians 12:9) 

However, I am (and we are) going through everyday with heavenly peace and a lot of comforts from brothers and sisters and friends.  Thank you for your prayers.

We had a musical program celebration gathering in the park this evening.  Many kids had a lot of fun running around in the park.  I recalled Lydia had a lot fun in this event same time last year.  Of course, the thought made my heart ache and made me sad.  Why wasn’t Lydia given the chance like other kids, just have fun and without much trouble, or simply still alive.  Then, I noticed Lydia’s three siblings were also having fun.  Yes, that is it.  Who am I, how can I argue with God.  God willing, then we may be able to live, and do this or do that.  May God’s name be glorified.

Often, we (even Christians) take for granted that we will get up tomorrow when we go to bed tonight.  And we will go to bed again tomorrow.  Without knowing that is all God’s grace, we are living like a fool (sorry, in my opinion, it’s a lot more foolish to reject God), and we are wasting our earthly life.

We should live for God, to please God, and use our time that way.
