October 26, 2006, Thursday

A question kept coming to my mind today.  What is the purpose of Lydia’s life on earth, only ten years, she was still growing, still learning.  Suddenly, her life stopped, exited the stage.  What is the point?

So short.  If we knew her life would be this short, would we raise her differently? In what way?  In which way so we would not have regret?

I thought and thought.  Then, I realized that is God’s grace that we did not know about it.  Otherwise, we would have so much regret.  Because how do we know what is the best to live, other than to live everyday like this is the last day of our earthly life, since we do not know what would happen tomorrow. (Ecclesiastes 3:14)

Everything is in God’s hand, and God, our heaven father, knows the best for us.

Therefore, a life with the length of one hundred years, or ten years, or one day, or still in the womb, all are precious.  Not more, not less, and extremely important in God’s eye.

The value and purpose of life is not how much earthly accomplishment we have (because for some, they never got chance), but how God view our lives.  Long or short, is it really matter?

Lydia’s life is short, is it unfortunately?

Our lives are longer, is it fortunately?

One thing I am sure.  We need to fear God, keep His commandment.  This is our duty.  (Ecclesiastes 12:13)

 

 

A school secretary shared with us that she showed Lydia’s web site to a student’s mother, a nonbeliever.  The mother came back next morning, and said to the secretary,

“You made me cry all night yesterday, I never realized the faith can be that beautiful.”

So the secretary was able to share with the mother more about the Christian faith.

Some shared with us that although they were raised in a Christian home.  However, into adulthood, they did not actively involve in any church, nor do they care to believe in God or not.  Lydia’s story made them think a lot, and how important to raise their children with the faith.

 

 

Everyday continues to be difficult for us.  A lot of pain and sorrow, just like I have joy and happiness.  Unless I deny joy and happiness, I would be vulnerable to pain and sorrow. 

That is OK, for I know my redeemer lives.  (Job 19:21)
