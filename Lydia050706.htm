May 7, 2006, Sunday

Lydia has performed all three shows of school musical play over the weekend. The play was so great. All the children, parents, relatives, and friends enjoyed the shows so much. As to us, while enjoy watching Lydia and many other children performing, not only so entertaining, we were extremely thankful to our God, for He performed the miracle in this play.

(Tell the truth, at first, the play staffs were thinking that maybe Lydia could just show up for a few seconds in one of the three shows. That would be great, and we were not even sure if that could happen.)

The entire cast started practicing the same week Lydia was diagnosed with brain tumor. For the past three months, the school play and Lydia’s fight with disease seem going in parallel (means they would not cross each other). As the school play getting in shape over time, Lydia has gone through so much, two brain surgeries, one spinal tap and port insertion surgery, scary CT and MRI results one after another one, hospital stays, radiation therapies, too many medicines, aches, pains, extreme discomforts, countless hospital visits…. Who would have thought (including ourselves, we did not think it’s possible and did not dare to dream about it) that Lydia would be back to the play and with her friends performed together.

At the end, the two lines intersected. Lydia joined the play. God’s way is really higher than ours.

An aunt came to watch the play. Whenever Lydia showed up on the stage, she could not help but crying. She knew what Lydia has been through. What appeared in front of her was a miracle, a live miracle…

Lydia received so much love and support from the school, entire play casts, staffs, families, and many others.  God’s love came in many wonderful forms.

Our God has mercy and great healing power. Our God is amazing. If you were with us during this journey, please join us to praise the Lord.

Oh, Lord, thank you….
