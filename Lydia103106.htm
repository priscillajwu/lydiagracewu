October 31, 2006, Tuesday

We do not celebrate Halloween, nor do we worship the dark side.

For years, in the Halloween evening, mom and girls would prepare many leaflets printed with “Jesus loves you”, and nice candies.  Whenever doorbell rang, the neighborhood kids came to treat-or-trick, the girls would rush to the door, open the door, and give them the leaflet and candies, and said “Jesus loves you”.  When the neighbor kids left, the girls would say, “God bless you”.

It was a lot of fun for all four girls.  But the younger they were, (recent years would be Lydia and Priscilla), the more excited they were.

Today, without Lydia, none of us felt doing the same routine.  It was a difficult day for us to get through.  It’s impossible to have fun while grieving and thinking of Lydia.  Therefore, we went out to eat with another family from the church.

The moment I arrived at the restaurant (we all, including Lydia, like this restaurant for their steak), I said to myself, “Wow, we have not been here for a long time.”  Indeed, ever since Lydia got sick, we seldom went to the restaurant, because Lydia’s immune system was low, and we did not want the risk.

Now, it seems our life could get back to what we used to do.  No, not really, the whole world is very different to me now.  I have much deeper perspective of it.  The lessons learned are precious, but the price to pay is too high, higher than I can afford.  Losing Lydia is worse than loss of my own life.  No exaggeration.  The pain is so severe, to go on a regular life takes a lot of effort.  However, since it’s from the Lord, I will take it, for I believe that He has full of mercy and grace.  And yes, God is good.  Otherwise, I won’t be able to sit here and typing…

The lesson is extremely important, for me, for my family, and for people who know us.  That is, to live is to fear God and keep His commandments.  Otherwise, life has no meaning.  Long or short, it doesn’t matter.  It’s not in our hand, but in God’s hand. 

 

“This happened for a good reason...” Lydia said, right after she was diagnosed with brain tumor.  This is how she trusted the Lord.
