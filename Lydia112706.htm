Monday, November 27, 2006

 

The day after Thanksgiving, Esther and I went to a Christian Store to get something.

When I came back, I asked Priscilla, “Would you like to see your first Christmas present this year or your are going to wait until the Christmas day?”

“It’s from Lydia.” I added.

“How could it be possible from Lydia?” Priscilla curiously asked me.

I explained it to her. Lydia and I actually went to the store before her last hospital stay. But it was not in stock. I told her that we could come back to buy it after her chemotherapy. But she never came out the hospital alive…

Her love for her sisters never could say it enough.

 

Last night, Priscilla has a hard time to fall asleep after a long Thanksgiving break. I tried to keep her company. She said she is lonely. I thought Deborah has moved in to sleep with her, it would help. Apparently, it is not. Deborah already fell asleep.

Priscilla said, “Lydia always tried to stay up with me, when I couldn’t fall asleep.”

“She would read me a book or talked to me something.”

“We even had made up some different kinds of knocking to present different meanings.”

This was the first time I heard about knocking story. These two sisters were really closely bonded.

Priscilla said it sadly; “I miss Lydia!”

“Yes, Mom miss her, too.” I cried badly.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

I found several artificial flowers, real flowers, and gift and card were left at Lydia’s gravesite today. We really appreciate people who put them there. We know our Lydia hasn’t been forgotten. It means a lot to us, thank you.
