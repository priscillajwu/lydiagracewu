October 17, 2006, Tuesday

Many parents probably have the same experience.

For years, mom and dad often mixed up four girls names.  Even now, we continued to do that once a while.  We would call one of the three girls, “Lydia”.  Then, realize we made mistake.  When that happened, girls did not say anything.  Of course, the moment is very sad and mom and dad just had to hold the tears.

 

Priscilla decorated her and Lydia’s room with some blankets, and made a canopy.  She and Lydia always found something new in their room.  Without Lydia, this time, mommy helped.

Priscilla curled up in it.  She had so much fun.  Everything looked so good and beautiful.  I kept feeling something wrong.  Well, that is, because Lydia is missing from this fun scene.

 

The world continues moving on fast.  Our grieving hearts continue heavy and painful.  During these sorrowful times, we have more time to pray, to read Scripture, and to feel what’s true peace in the Lord.  I move slowly in many aspects.  I am incapable of moving fast anyway.

 

One colleague reminded me to count many blessings I have from the Lord.  I do.  I have been doing that for a long time, before Lydia got ill.  With suffering and grieving, feeling blessed can not replace the sorrow.  However, one thing for sure is that I tightly hold on and grasp the Lord momentarily.
