April 29, 2007, Sunday

I think I had one of the worst weeks since Lydia passed away, very painful and sorrowful.

I am surprised that I was able to get through this week without people around me noticed much about it.  I guess I had to.  Otherwise, it would have been even more difficult.

The weekend was full of activities, definitely more than the usual weekends.  And it started early Friday that Deborah left out of town with school orchestra and I went to high school as a guest speaker in a language class (or third flat tire incident within one month one day earlier…).

Then, all the weekend chores, led bible study, prepared and taught Sunday school, took kids to activities.  And on top of that, high school prom was in this weekend.  My house was gathering place for several couples.  So, a lot of preparation for mom and dad.  I had to cut grass (for them to take nice pictures).  And then lawn mower broke.  I did fix it and finish mowing before the arrival of exciting high school kids.  After sending them off to take limo, all the restaurants were so crowded that we have to drive around to find a place to eat and relax.  We also had to wait for the high schoolers to come back from prom and then sending them off back to high school for after prom activities.  And that was late in the night.  Not any easier today, church meeting in the afternoon after tiring morning, and air condition in my car suddenly broke.  Then, attending concert with Deborah performing Beethoven’s No. 5 symphony.  Of course, we were late because of downloading the prom pictures before we left home.  In the evening, the printer broke so kids could not print nice copy of their homework due tomorrow.

 

However, think back, are these burdens or blessings.  For the troubled mind, they were definitely burdens and could drive me crazy and irritable.  Thank the Lord, He kept reminding me that these were blessings.  Indeed, they ARE fun stuff for normal and sane people (we are talking about kids attending prom and performing symphony No.5).  And these activities kept the family going.  Although sometimes I was just auto-pilot, I guess my problem is that I still wonder why Lydia was not in the orchestra playing cello.

 

Mom made a comment over the weekend that the younger two girls required a lot of attention than before.  Then, all of sudden, we realized that Lydia, although being a kid herself and need parents’ attention.  But she was giving a lot of attention to the siblings.  She helped taking care of siblings and keeping them busy, relieving mom and dad.  Now, nobody could help.  Attention giving job came back to mom and dad.
