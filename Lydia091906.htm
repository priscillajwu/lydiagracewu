September 19, 2006, Tuesday

Finally, mom sent out some thank you cards today.  It has been very difficult for mom to write the cards.  The thank you cards printed with Lydia’s just turned nine years old picture with pink dress.  She took one year ago.  Many people have seen it which was displayed during the farewell celebration party…

 

In the evening, I went to pick up Deborah from school.  I passed the route which I took Lydia to double accelerated math class for a full year.  Old memory came to me, and I was totally torn.

“It is so difficult.  It’s way too difficult.”  I cried and shouted.  I screamed out loud her name again and again.  I missed her desperately.  My heart was bleeding.  I was in so much sorrow and pain.  My shirt sleeve was so wet after wiping out my tears (I was surprised).

Of course, I needed to stop before arriving school.  Deborah was not aware of anything.  While talking with her in the car, I could feel the lower abdominal pain.  It’s not a good sign.  This emotional agony will eventually hurt me physically. 

 

We are grieving terribly.  Mom and I had trouble to sleep, especially mom.  Mom would wake up middle of the night every day and mourn.  It would take her a while before she could go back to sleep.

 

 “Does Lydia know we are grieving this bad?  If she knows, then she would be very sad.  But there should not be any sadness where she is now.  So, what she is enjoying now has to be so good that any sorrow could be reversed.”  I said to mom, “Or, because there is no time limit for her.  Maybe she is now meeting us (our future us) and is so happy.”

Mom did not say anything.  I know I think too much…

 

Mom recalled that, recently (after Lydia got ill) mom wanted Lydia to clean up some of her stuff.  (Lydia liked to keep all her stuff.  She would not throw away things.)  Lydia was very upset and said, “If I die, are you going to throw away my old stuff?”

Now mom looked at Lydia’s stuff, this conversation they had hurts mommy extremely…

 

We need to pray hard for God’s help to relieve our pain and sorrow.

 

We are slowly coming up with some ideas how to continue sharing her story to many others.  May God continue using Lydia’s life for His Kingdom.

 

May God’s name be glorified.
