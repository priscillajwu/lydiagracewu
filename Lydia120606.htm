December 6, 2006, Wednesday

Excerpt from a long message sent to Esther a couple of days ago from her friend at school,

“Esther, I've been kind of hesitant to talk about Lydia with you. I have never told you, but I probably have some of the highest respect for you out of anybody I know. I have read almost all of your father's and mother's journals and it just stuns me beyond comprehension. I can't imagine how painful it must be, and I know you hear that all the time but it is really beyond what I know. If you don’t want me to bring it up, I understand completely.

……

Esther… I have never told anybody, but Lydia, in many ways, saved me. When I read about her life, when I compared myself to her, when I learned all these things it changed me. I had finally found Christ after the longest time she just put a new perspective on me. She has made me appreciate life, put my faith to shame, broken me in half when I thought I had it all figured out. She has helped me through temptation where I thought I could not resist but I thought about her resistance. I cant describe all of these in words, but I do know she put new life in me. I never met her, but I know I will some day. I have found the directions to her grave, and I think its about time I thanked her.

……”

Esther and her friend were willing to share this message with mom and dad.  We were very touched.  Thank you.

 

Pain and sorrow continue to be the daily routine (more frequent than eating and drinking).  But we thank God that He brought us a lot of grace and comfort through all kinds of ways, EVERYDAY.  Otherwise, it would be hard to go on.

Therefore, for God, for the family, for brothers and sisters in Christ, and for all the people who know about Lydia, with God’s grace, we can be faithful, and be strong with the strength from the Lord.

 

Mom has been a great counselor recently to some friends who are facing the adversity in life.  Since they knew what mom has been through, it meant so much to them, and brought them a lot of comfort.

Praise the Lord.
