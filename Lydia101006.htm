October 10, 2006, Tuesday

For whatever reason, I got on the computer and started to look at Lydia’s pictures back when she was ill.  Big mistake.  I was so sorrowful in front of computer and caused lower abdominal pain.  Mom came over, and saw what I was doing.  She asked me not to look.  She could not handle it when Lydia’s pictures were on the computer screen.

 

During Lydia’s illness, mom often encouraged Lydia,

“We take this one year, out of our ordinary life.  Let God shape us so that we can be the more suitable tool for God in the future.”

And Lydia took that seriously and deeply believed in it.  Although under tremendous suffering, her spirit has been very strong.

Now, Lydia passed the test.  It was shorter than we thought, only seven months.  And also, no more suffering in earthly life for Lydia.

The problem is mom and dad.  We seem to be able to pass those seven months all right.  Although difficult, with God’s grace, we got it through.  However, right now, we have tremendous difficulty to live the life without Lydia.

Children, we thought, are the gifts from the Lord.  Actually, He can take away whenever He wants.  We, as the parents, are helpless, not able to keep Lydia one minute longer.

We thought children belong to God, also belong to us.  Well, not really.  They only belong to God.  Although they are the heritage from God (Psalm 127:3), there is no guarantee the heritage will always be there.

Mom said,

“We were ready to fight with Lydia no matter how long it takes, how difficult it is.  But we were not ready for losing her.”

It seems that it’s mom and dad need to be continuously shaped by the Lord.
