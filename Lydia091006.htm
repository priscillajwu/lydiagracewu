September 10, 2006, Sunday

Since our pastor was out of town, I had a chance to give a sermon in today’s Sunday service.

It was very difficult for me since it’s only 20 days since Lydia passed away.  I did not have the strength and was not ready to do the job both mentally and physically.  I had to try very hard to get myself together and prepare the message while doing my regular engineer job and with the severely grieving heart.  However, with God’s grace, I was able to do it.  (2 Timothy 4:2).

In the middle of sermon, I choked up and froze while talking about Lydia.  I believed it was about two minutes (it felt like forever while standing there).  I got so emotional and just not able to continue.  After urging brothers and sisters praying for me and wiped away my tears, I was able to finish to the end without problem.  It was embarrassing, yes, but praise the Lord that I had chance to preach God’s Words.

I shared the “Sun of Righteousness” (Malachi 4:2) which will rise with healing at the Day of the Lord.

Yes God will heal, and our greatest comfort during this difficult time is from the Lord that, with His promise, we will meet Lydia again.

I also shared that we should build up our faith to stand the trials (1 Peter 4:12,13).  Since the curveball in life will happen (just the matter of time).  We need to be prepared with the help from our Lord, Christ Jesus.

 

 

In the afternoon, Priscilla came down from upstairs crying.  I thought there was something serious.

“Mom, I don’t know what to wear…”

Such a little thing!  I was a little angry, then, tears came to my eyes...

Before, Lydia often picked clothes for Priscilla to wear.  Well, now Lydia is gone.  Priscilla just needs to grow up to take care of herself.

Lydia had the best artistic sense about clothes.  We often asked her opinion when we could not decide what to wear or what to purchase in the store.  We trusted her judgment.

Too bad, and it’s sad that, we all need to learn how to dress since Lydia is no longer around to help us...
