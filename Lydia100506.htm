October 5, 2006, Thursday

A hallway conversation with one Christian colleague from work,  (Isn’t that nice?  Because of Lydia, I realized there are many brothers and sisters in the Lord at work place.)

“My observation, among the people who knew us,” I shared with him, “the Christians were encouraged, and more dedicated to the Lord.  However, for the non-believers, they seemed drifting further away.”

“Don’t be so quick to pass judgment,” He told me, “Lydia’s story is like a seed planted in their hearts.  Maybe years from now, the seed will grow.”

Yes, indeed, our God does amazing work.

 

Today, I introduced myself to a new friend.  I told him I have four daughters, one passed away several weeks ago at age of ten from cancer.  Then, I realized I could witness God at that moment about our faith and hope to reunite with Lydia in the future.  Yes, Lydia lives.  Her story continues being a great way to share God’s love and eternal hope.

 

“May our Lord Jesus Christ himself and God our Father, who loved us and by his grace gave us eternal encouragement and good hope, encourage your hearts and strengthen you in every good deed and word.”  2 Thessalonians 2:16-17.

 

 

At Lydia’s grave, I noticed a thin patch of one to two inches of grass.  I recalled the manager from the cemetery told us that grass would not grow until next spring.  So, I was surprised and excited to see the grass coming out of the soil.  Thank God for a warmer than usual October weather, and a lot of rain last few days.  When I saw the grass, it reminded me that God is here with us.  God is always here, whether I am aware or not…
