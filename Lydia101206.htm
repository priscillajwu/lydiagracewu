October 12, 2006, Thursday

I called home from office in the late afternoon.  Usually kids were all home from school by then.  Esther answered the phone.  I heard a laughing and high pitch voice in the background.  It sounded just like Lydia.  Really, just like Lydia’s voice.  It made me so sad.  For years, when I called home around the same time, Lydia would be talking in the background, no matter who answered the phone.  I could always hear her.  Sometimes, Lydia answered the phone, and said, “Hi, daddy”, and then continue talking to me with a lot of laughs.  (Now think back, she seems to laugh the most among all four girls.)

Of course, today, I found out right away that it was Priscilla talking in the background.  She speaks much slower than Lydia.

Don’t I remember Lydia already passed away?

Life is short, either ten years or eighty years.

Being alive is so precious.  We got to do our best to live.  Never take for granted, never lazy, never give up…

 

I realized Mom and I talked a lot nowadays, more than ever.  We talked, shared, discussed, cried, grieved, and recalled many things about Lydia.  The most importantly, we have about the same level of spiritual faith.  Our thoughts, our feelings, our belief, our philosophy, and our value, are very similar.  It’s easy to communicate, and help and comfort each other deeply in our hearts.

It’s so important for both husband and wife to trust in the Lord.  We thank God for such a wonderful blessing.
