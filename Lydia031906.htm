March 19, 06

My prayer heroes,

In order to treat and reduce Lydia’s headache and nausea, she has been taking quite a few different kinds of medicine over the weekend: steroid, pain killers, anti-nauseous medicine, for constipation, and for stomach…  I believe she is a little bit better compared with when she just came home from hospital on Friday.  Thanks for your prayers.  Praise the Lord.

Over last several days, we had a lot of visitors, felt like non-stopping.  We want to thank all for the love and care.  Sorry for those who did not get to see Lydia when she was not really feeling well and rested in her bed.  Although over-whelmed with visitation, we understood it’s the love of our Lord, through many brothers and sisters, uncles and aunts, teachers, and friends…  We were very happy for you to visit, but it did get Lydia a bit stressed out.  And we were really worried about her getting any infection.  Sorry for those who called and we asked you not to come.  It was not easy for us.

 

In the evening, all of sudden, Lydia burst into tear and said, “I can’t hold to myself anymore, I have to tell you.”

“I am scared that I won’t able to live to see myself turning 10 years old.”  She continued.  (She was exactly 9 and half when she was diagnosed with brain tumor on Feb. 6.)

This shocked us and made mom and I very very sad.  A big lump was in my throat and my eyes quickly turned watery when I walked over and held her.

“Of course you will. Why?”  I asked, probably with a lot of worries and puzzle on my face. “When did you start to think this way?”

“Since beginning, I was really scared.” Lydia said.

“Doctors said the cure rate for this disease is very high.” I told her. “I thought you heard that in the hospital.”

Mom on the side added, “Besides, no matter what doctor said, we have God with us, He gave me the promise of ‘totally cured’, remember that ?”

Lydia nodded, and said, “I am just really scared.”

“Nothing to be scared.”  I said, “From now on, I want you tell us everything in you mind, OK?”

“I was embarrassed, and I did not want you to feel low….”  She said.

Well, I was feeling low for the entire night because of this.  We had more talks after that.  Why there are suffering to many people, she thought about the future…

At these moments, the physical pain became easier to deal with.  The emotional pain for Lydia was more than I thought.

Jean and I talked privately afterwards and felt very sad, also shed some tears.  All of these are probably normal, but what can I say.  Is it easy? No.  Again, I asked for God’s comfort and mercy.  We are so weak, and have so little control of everything.  I need to ask for God’s almighty power and His wisdom to get through all of these ...

 

In Him,

Simon
