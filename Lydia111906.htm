Sunday, November 19, 2006

 

This morning before our own church Sunday worship, we got invited to speak to a youth Sunday class. It was a great experience for us. It was also such a rewarding experience for us to see how people would be touched by Lydia’s life. We wish we could have more opportunity to share to more groups of people, if God will open the door.

 

 

 

A few days ago, one of Lydia’s fourth grade teachers shared this with us … …

 

It isn't as though I need a reminder to think of that magical little girl. But there it is on my board in my classroom. Last year, I asked the 4th graders if anyone would consider donating their money design projects that I might use it in the future as an example. Selfless and willing, Lydia gave me hers. It is one of two that I was given last year. Other kids really wanted to keep theirs, for which I can't fault them. After their hard work, it is rightfully theirs! But Lydia was willing to help others in extending that offer of using hers for the future. Last week I got out those examples to share with this year's 4th graders since they are ready to take their knowledge and create their own currency. When I showed them the examples they were excited and impressed. When I showed them Lydia's there was a collective sigh- they all held their breath and just LOOKED. So many of those little ones knew Lydia from church or Symmes Players. Then the praise started at her ingenuity, creativity, and problem solving ability. The connections to the things she liked as she chose her symbols (it was designed for "Redville," her favorite color - as those who knew informed the others). It went on and on! They held it up as a stellar example. And it is, there is no denying it. We talked about the aesthetic quality of the presentation. How well written her explanation is. I just think they were touched to have a piece of her there with us to help us learn. Does that surprise you? Always a teacher! It doesn't surprise me. And so she's there when I turn around. A little piece, but a special piece with a very tangible memory of a time I cherish that I knew a wonderful girl who taught ME so much. Thank you. Again and again.

 

Thank you Lord, for using our beloved Lydia to teach us the life lessons you want us to learn, thank you!
