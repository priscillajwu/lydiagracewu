October 15, 2006, Sunday

Grandma is leaving.  We took her to the shopping mall to buy some stuff for her to bring home.

Last time we went to the shopping mall, Lydia was with us.  Although Lydia was ill, that was a happy time.  All four girls scrolled a little bit (as much as Lydia could handle).

Today, the place is the same, packed with a lot of people.  But where is Lydia?

“Can I have this?”  Priscilla asked me with a scarf in her hand.

I thought about Lydia.  Only until she got sick, she was able to get me buy something she wanted.  For years, whenever the girls asked me for something, nine of ten, I would say, No, you don’t need that, or it doesn’t look good.  So, gradually, they were scared of asking me.  They asked mommy instead.  Daddy really does not know the girls’ stuff.

One out of ten, I would buy four pieces for each one of them.  That is what I felt today.  OK, I told them it’s that we could not afford, you just could not get whatever you want.  But this scarf, it’s very special.  Thinking of Lydia, I just had to get it for them.

I bought three pieces of scarf.  Three?  What a weird number.  Should be four…

 

Tonight, Sunday night, it’s an enjoyable night, and another very sorrowful night.  We played some games together, prayed together.  We talked a lot.  And mom and dad just wept too many times.

 

If life is this tough, why bother to live this life.  There must be something higher, higher than this, higher than what we can see or imagine.  And thank the Lord, God promised us the eternal life with Him in heaven.  Current life is only preparing us for the next life to be with the Lord.  Otherwise, what is the point?

 

By the way, today was the first time for Deborah to be the pianist in the adult worship.  Mom was worship leader.  (Too bad I had to teach Sunday school, not able to be there while Deborah served as the pianist for the very first time.)  Afterwards, a sister came to tell mom with tear in her eyes.  She saw the faith and willingness to serve from our family.  “If it were me,” she said, “I would still hide in the house, not able to get out of the house yet, not to mention to serve the Lord, even in front of the congregation.”

Well, it’s not that we do not want to take a break from serving Him.  Serve the Lord is why we are here.  Otherwise, what’s the point to breath and we will be wasting the oxygen.  Yes, grieving is tough, difficult to endure, and going on regular life is challenging.  But we do not stop serving Him.

God willing, then we may have tomorrow to serve Him, to go to school, to work, to love people, and to save souls for the kingdom of God.
