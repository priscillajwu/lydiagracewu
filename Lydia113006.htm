November 30, 2006, Thursday

An excerpt of email received yesterday from a dear sister in Christ.

“……

Every day I think about you and your family.  To be more precise, I never stop thinking or talking about Lydia.  I share with my family after reading your updated information about you and your family.  I share with my bible study fellowship about Lydia's strong faith and hope.  Thank God for giving me plenty of opportunities to share Lydia's life, which definitely glorifies our Lord.  Thank you for setting good example for us.  Lydia and your family have definitely taught us many valuable lessons.  How can we forget Lydia?

“

How can we forget Lydia…  That would be an impossible task until I meet Lydia again

 

During Lydia’s treatment, we had a plan to have a vacation this Christmas break, to celebrate that Lydia gets through the harsh radiation and chemotherapy treatments.  I promised Lydia that I would grant her wish wherever she wanted to go or do.  She was really excited about it and very looking forward to it.  (If Lydia were still alive, her chemotherapy would be finished about one month ago.)

Tonight, mom and three girls were looking for the vacation package and ideas.  The girls were really excited.  They showed me the web sites, printed out the information, with a lot of fun and excitement.

Be frankly, my heart just ached terribly.  My heart was so saddened even though I said to them, “Good job, keep looking for the good stuff.”

“Why are your eyes so red?”

“Oh, probably because I stared at computer too much.” I replied.

I am not sure if I can survive to take the family vacation without Lydia.  That would be so strange.  It would be so heart breaking.  How can we have fun?

Also, I am not sure if I can survive if I stay home and grieve for so many days during the Christmas break.  Only several days during Thanksgiving break were difficult enough for me.

To reach out to people with needs would be something we usually liked to do.  But the thought that Lydia would not be part of it, again, hurts and unbearable.

People suggested me to get busy.  I told them, I lost the sense of feeling busy or not (although my everyday life is also stuffed with many things like everybody else).  So, that does not help.  Being busy or not, I still miss Lydia terribly, days and nights, awake or asleep (a lot of dreams).

 

Only prayers and Scripture reading do help. The Lord gives me strength through it.  He has mercy and grace.

I should do a lot of spiritual book reading and prayers during this Christmas season.
