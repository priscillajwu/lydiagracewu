November 4, 2006, Saturday

The groundbreaking ceremony of our new church building was held today.

There were many brothers and sisters, and children gathered together for the celebration.  Great hymns were sung, wonderful prayers were said, and group pictures were taken.  Little kids were especially excited…

Lydia has been wondering when was this day going to arrive.  Well, about two and half months after she passed away.  She did not live long enough and missed this celebration.

She will continue to miss many more ceremonies from the church or important moments from the family.

I felt bad that she was not able to be part of all of these.  However, there should be a lot of much more significant celebrations and ceremonies in heaven with our heavenly father.  And Lydia was ahead of us to be part of those.

How poor we are to be stuck here, and could not even imagine how great it is in the heaven.

 

 

Mom met a friend, who is a mother, an unbeliever, was sad and cried terribly simply because her son went to college and she missed him.

In front of my wife?  And Jean was trying to comfort her.

When I first heard about it, how pathetic was what I felt about the situation.

Later, I realized I was wrong, how could I judge others using my own current experience.  I was not her, and she was not me.  Her sorrow was authentic.  The bottom line is that she needs the Lord, needs the faith to help her cope with it.  And this is probably a good time for her to seriously think about it.

 

 

In the afternoon, Priscilla was lying down comfortably and reading a book.  When I looked at her, the book covered half of her face.  For a quick second, I thought I saw Lydia.  Priscilla does look like Lydia from certain angle.  Of course, that brought tears to my eyes…
