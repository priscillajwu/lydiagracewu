December 5, 2006, Tuesday

I took a health survey at work today.  We did that every year.  It includes physical, mental, diet, activity, stress level, family history, and many other health related questions.  It was pretty thorough.  I used to score pretty high in the previous years.  Without surprise, I scored terribly low this year.  I don’t know how they score, but I believe my sorrow and grief pull me down significantly.

Well, what I don’t like is that the program put me in the high risk health category.  I thought a lot of Americans are in high risk because of over weight.  I don’t have that problem.  I am in high risk pretty much because I lost my daughter (there was a question “Did you experience the death of loved one this past year?”).  This health survey is pretty smart, and gave me a laughter, and then an unspoken sadness.

 

Mom and I went to the grieving support group again tonight.  Again, very few people showed up, only one person other than us.  It made me feel we are the only people who are grieving.  Well, of course not.  As matter of fact, God showed us that He is truly the source of comfort.

 

I am somewhat tired of saying, “Good”, “OK”, “Fine”, when people greeted me, either in person, on the phone, email, at work, or at church.  I have to wear mask in order not to make people around me feel bad.  It’s not the best thing I would like to do.

Also, people said wrong words out of the good intention.  When I heard it, I just smiled.

 

Although life is difficult, I thank God that we are still millions times better than people who do not believe in God, or refuse the salvation (which means their enjoyment or hope is only in the current earthly live, we know, that is so impossible, vague and insignificant.)

 

The Lord is the only way.
