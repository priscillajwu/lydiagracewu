November 8, 2006, Wednesday

Finally, the marker on Lydia’s grave has been installed.  So, it will be permanent.  Unless the end time arrives before I die; otherwise, it will be most likely this way for the rest of my life.

It is so heartbreaking simply looking at the marker, and to think that my dear daughter’s earthly body rest here.  The pain and sorrow hit me so hard while I was standing there.

It’s so weird to see the difference between two dates on the marker is only ten years apart.  Markers surrounding Lydia’s were like 70 to 90 some years old when they died.  Lydia was youngest by too many decades in that area of cemetery.

After taking the picture, I cried loudly while walking (stumbling) toward my car.  (Nobody was there anyway.)  Many times in the past three months, while I was at Lydia’s grave, I cried out so loud, as though God and Lydia could hear me from the sky.  Actually, I thank God for being able to do that whenever I need to.

 

There is a little tree right behind Lydia’s grave.  It’s about 15 to 20 feet tall.  The leaves have changed the color.  And it is …., yes, RED, Lydia’s favorite color.  I thank God so much for that.  Just like little thing like this, He comforts me in many ways.  Although I do not know why God took Lydia so early, He is still my shepherd, my Lord, my refuge, my   , everything.

 

Thank God for the promise that we will meet Lydia again in heaven with the Lord.  Without this ultimate hope, it’s hard to live another second on earth.
