September 1, 2006, Friday

For last several days, the flowers in Lydia’s grave would be gone the day after we put it. We did not know what happened. Today, we were pretty sure that the flowers were eaten by the wild animals, probably deers. I was quite upset in the first place. Later, I thought, well, that is OK (Lydia’s always said that for things we do not have the control over). Maybe we’ll just have to use the artificial flowers.

It’s Friday again. It’s the first Friday night Lydia was not around. (Last Friday, many people came to visit, I was standing next to Lydia’s casket.) I recalled many good memories about Friday nights. I was home with Lydia alone. We would do many things together. Tonight, I wept several times pretty badly. Grandma (dad’s mom) told me not to think about her. I am not sure if she knows what she is talking about.

I and Lydia played a lot of games on those Friday nights. She was very good and graceful. Whenever she lost, she would say she was not good at it yet (never too upset about it). She would like to try it again. Only a few games more, she would be able to beat me. Then, she would said,

"Did you do that on purpose to let me win to make me happy?"

"No, not at all, I am playing it seriously." I said pretty surely, "You just learn things very fast."

Yes, she gave her best in everything she did.

Recall back in August 10, Lydia used a walker to walk from her room to the vending machine in the hospital. That was the last time in her life to walk by herself. Walking along the hall way, with mom and dad, doctor and nurses around, with her arms and legs shaking along each step, she kept saying,

"Yes, I need to stay strong."

"I need to get better."

"God will help me."

"I will get better."

"I can do it, I can do it …"

Back then, I cheered for her. The doctor and nurses’ eyes were watery. Now, only thought about it would get me totally torn.

Many people said Lydia is truly inspirational. But I wish she would have complained more, or whined more, or did not try that hard to fight the disease. That would make me less in sorrow, or less guilty.

Of course I am proud of her. But recalling her pure hope and faith while she was terribly suffering, just made me cry.

And why she was that strong. Well, it must be from God, and because of the prayers of many…
