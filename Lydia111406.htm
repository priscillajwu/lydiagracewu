November 14, 2006, Tuesday

The daylight gets shorter and shorter.

My daily visit to Lydia’s grave after work just got darker and darker.  I left work later than usual today.  By the time I arrived the cemetery, it’s almost totally dark.  The car headlight helped me to park in the right place.  However, I have to try a little bit harder to get to Lydia’s grave.

It was very dark and cold, and there was no light in the cemetery.  I could barely see Lydia’s face in the picture on the marker.  So I touched the oval picture, rubbed gently and cried.  I was in deep sorrow.

I thought to myself, since it’s so dark, would I see any spirit?  Could I see Lydia’s spirit?  If not, an angel coming to comfort me would be nice…

I waited for a while.  No, nothing I could see, other than the dark night.

A little bit disappointed, I stumbled back to my car, still crying…

At the moment like this, only the Lord was with me.  It’s nice that I could cry to the Lord in the dark.  I was not able to cry at home, at work, or in front of people, that would make people uneasy, not in front of my girls, not even in front of mom, that would make them sad…

I thank God for the dark night grieving at Lydia’s grave.  Somehow, I was comforted.

Matthew 5:4, was never special to me before.  Now it is definitely one of my favorite verses.
