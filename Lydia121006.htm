December 10, 2006, Sunday

There was a Christmas celebration at church presented by all the children today.  It felt good and fun to watch all the presentations celebrating the birth of the Christ, as well as the Christmas spirits.

However, first year without Lydia being there was just awkward, painful, and heartbreaking.  Anyway, she is totally out of everything on earth.  Wherever mom and dad and sisters or friends were, she just could not make it.  She could no longer participate, only her spirits though.

 

One thing kept in my mind these two days.  It’s the “perception”.  How I perceive Lydia’s death.  I am not sure.  It was so complex and not easy to figure out.  I am continuing seeking for the wisdom from the Lord.

 

Priscilla bought some little Christmas trees (red, gold and green) using her own money.  She wanted to put them on the Lydia’s gravesite.  She also took a used toothbrush to clean Lydia’s grave marker.  It got muddy easily since it’s flat on the ground.

Priscilla still could not sleep by herself. Nowadays, either Deborah or mom would sleep with her.  It’s sad around the bedtime that she would have to beg for somebody to sleep with her.  Sometimes, Deborah fell asleep early, Priscilla would not be able to fall asleep.  She would have to read by herself until…, I don’t know how late.  Sadly that her buddy sister is dead.  Is that fair to Priscilla? NO.  Is it fair to Lydia, NO.  Is it fair to any of us in the family? NO.

We just need to have the faith and believe that our God could not be wrong.  When we see Him, He will have all the answers for us.  For now, He is still the best and ultimate source of the comfort for us…
