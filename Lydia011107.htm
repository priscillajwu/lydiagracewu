January 11, 2007, Thursday

 

“How are you doing?”  A lot of people asked me with care and concern.

“OK.”  I would smile back.

“That’s good.” Friends were happy then.

 

Actually when I said OK, I meant, “Not so good, sometimes terrible.”  But I did not want to get people upset.  “OK”, “I am all right”, with a smile will do the trick.  If I acted my true self to others, I would be the most dislikable person in the world.  People want happiness; few appreciate sufferings.

 

 

When in the meeting (at work or at church), I would do a lot of listening nowadays.  A lot of argument really did not interest me.  Don’t they know life is so short?  Why are we wasting time talking so much irrelevant stuff?

 

In a way, I am afraid to be alone.  But when with people around, I wish people could simply leave me alone, especially insensitive ones…

 

Really, spend time in grieving, is better for the spirit and soul.

 

“It is better to go to a house of mourning than to go to a house of feasting, for death is the destiny of every man; the living should take this to heart.

Sorrow is better than laughter, because a sad face is good for the heart.

The heart of the wise is in the house of mourning, but the heart of fools is in the house of pleasure.” Ecclesiastes 7:2-4

 

These three verses were so true and with a lot of wisdom of life and eternity.  Before, I understood what they meant, but did not truly know it.  Now, I know better.  Sadly, people are shallow enough to read these verses with disgust or ignorance.

 

 

My hair has grown back to the length before I shaved the head with Lydia last April.  Looking at myself in the mirror would give me a lot of aches.  I was hoping I could grow hair together with Lydia.  That would be a lot of joy and fun.  Unfortunately, this wish to grow hair together is too much of luxury for me and Lydia.
