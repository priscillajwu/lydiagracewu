October 7, 2006, Saturday

An excerpt of an email received today from a ten years old boy who was in Lydia’s Sunday school class.  (He mentioned about AWANA which is the name of our church children program.  Children memorize bible verses and learn bible lessons every week.)

“I got to know Lydia since we were in 2nd grade at church’s Sunday School and AWANA, and I consider her as my good friend. She cared about people and she was always willing to help others. When we were in Sunday School class, it seems that Lydia knew all the answers, and was eager to share her thoughts. She was a happy girl, and she laughed a lot. Another thing I remember her is the way she talks, and I could never figure out how she could talk that fast, and how much she has to say. At AWANA games, Lydia could run as fast as many of the boys, and was helping her teammates get ready when she finished her turn. When I asked my 5 year old brother what he remembered about Lydia, He said, Lydia tied my shoes.

Since Sparky class, we were at the same AWANA class every week. Lydia always tried her best to memorize many Bible verses, and that inspired me to memorize as much as possible as well. We tried to see who can finish the book first.   She was always positive and cheered people up, when they were down. She always was determined to do her best. She would participate in the games and not anticipate.

Lydia’s life is short, but Jesus has made BEAUTIFUL things in her life and through her. I’m thankful that I had the opportunity to get to know her. She inspired and touched a lot of people. Her spirit still lives on. This September, I was invited to be a guest pianist to perform at Northern Kentucky University’s 2006- 2007 season opening concert. The piece I played was Bach’s F Minor concerto. This concerto was hard for me. While I was practicing everyday, I became so frustrated that I wanted to quit. But my mom asked me, “What would Lydia do?”  I thought that even in the most difficult situations, she never gave up. I was encouraged by her spirit and kept on practicing. Bach was a fine Christian. For each piece he composed, he would write “To God be the Glory”. About 300 years ago, Bach wrote this concerto. Today, I had this opportunity to play it on stage. I tried to play Lydia’s story through the music, because I was truly inspired by her courage, love, hope, and most importantly, her faith to God. After the concert, a lady told me, “You played very well. Bach would have been very happy.” My mom told me that God would be happy too. I also believed that Lydia would be glad to hear that her story inspired people through this piece. May God’s name be glorified.

……”

 

“What would Lydia do?”  I asked that question many times myself.  With God’s grace, prayers of many people around the globe, a ten year-old child has lived an amazing life.  Started from late January since her headache began, during her last seven months of life, through her suffering, she showed her ultimate love for Jesus, people, and life itself.

“My life is forever changed.  Thank you for sharing Lydia’s story...”  Many people said or wrote that to us.

Halleluiah, Praise the Lord.

 

And I continue praying very hard that God help us and give us the strength to live with the pain and sorrow of the loss of Lydia.
