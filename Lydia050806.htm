May 8, 2006, Monday

Lydia did not go to school today, and stuck at home for the entire day. After I came back from work, I saw her face really pale. So, I dragged her out to go to Costco for grocery shopping, thinking at least she could get some walking and exercise a little bit.

At Costco, I bought two churros (the sweet cinnamon twist), one for mom and one for Lydia. (Usually we don’t get those. Only at special occasions and it’s a special treat.) So when I handed the churros to Lydia. She was really happy. Several minutes later, mom already finished hers for a while. Lydia still had a long piece, almost the same length of original. Although she pretended that she still enjoyed it, and continued eating it (very small tiny bites each time). I knew she could not eat and had hard time to swallow.

It’s so frustrating. How can I get her to eat? I though she would like it...

At night, when I accompanied Lydia while mom is sorting through the super thick stack of hospital bills, Lydia said,

"I wish I can gain weight, back to my original weight before the chemo therapy."

"But you are not eating enough." I said, "Besides, you only got three weeks to go."

"I know it’s hard. But I really want to." Lydia said wishfully.

Lydia told me that Priscilla is already several pounds heavier than her. It was just the opposite only a few months ago. I can see that being lighter than her younger sister is not something pleasant to Lydia.

I really need to pray hard for Lydia to gain some strength and weight and eat more.

Before Lydia went to take shower, she almost forgot she still have the hat on. She said,

"The hat is like my hair."

"I can see that and feel the same way since I don’t have hair either." I said while rubbing my bald head.

"I don’t know why did you do that." Lydia said with smile.

"Why I shaved my head? Don’t you like it! How about I grow it back?" I said.

"No, you can wait until my chemo therapies finish." Lydia laughed louder.

Like father like daughter. It’s the rare occasion that father and daughter could have the same hair style.

Thank the Lord for that.
