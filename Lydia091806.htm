September 18, 2006, Monday

Four weeks have gone by after Lydia left us to be with the Lord.

 

Tonight, mom asked me,

“Are we feeling better now?”

“No, I am not.  If not worse.”  I shook my head.  At the same time, mom also shook her head and started crying badly.

 

Every morning mom would wake up Priscilla (while everybody else in the family have gone to school or work) with both eyes red with a lot of tears.  For four weeks, mom would hold Priscilla tightly first thing in the morning.  Looking at mom’s eyes, Priscilla did not say anything.

Often, when girls came home in the afternoon.  They would find out mom’s eyes also red with tears.

Priscilla said to us tonight,

“You don’t have to cry every time you think of Lydia.”

“No, of course not.  Otherwise, I would be crying all day long.”  I told her.

 

Although many people who have seen us mentioned that they were amazed how well we are doing.  I am not sure if they just said something nice or that was true.  However, I don’t feel I am doing all right at all.  My sorrow and grief is just way over I can handle.  And I believe mom is worse than me.  And she did not show it when with others except with me or with the girls.

 

Mom said,

“Lydia is already with the Lord, and we have done our best for her to achieve that in only ten years.  And that is also the ultimate goal for the rest of our lives.  She did it a lot sooner than us.  So, why are we so sad?”

“We just miss her.  We would like her still be with us.”  I said.

“That is selfish though.” Esther said on the side.

 

No matter how we rationalize it, we will not feel better.  The emotion can not be comforted by the reasoning.  Only the Spirit can help. 

However, I am still learning it.  Not good at it at all.

I can only pray for God’s mercy to help us go through everyday…
