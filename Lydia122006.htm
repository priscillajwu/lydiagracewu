December 20, 2006, Wednesday

I took off from work last three days to be with mom (while all three girls are in school).  This is the first time after Lydia passed away that mom and dad spent several days together.  I am amazed to find out mom is coping the grief better than me.  She has been very busy with schools and girls’ activities.  She also got a lot of support from school staffs, teachers, and other parents.

I, a grieving father, have been feeling awkward wherever I went.  But with mom to do her daily chores, it seems comforting enough simply talking with mom’s circle of friends.

 

This is the last day of school of the year.  Esther invited several school friends over in the evening.  They had a lot of fun and laughter.  To tell the truth, my ears could not handle too much laughs nowadays.  (Before Lydia got sick, for years, I always enjoyed kids’ having birthday parties at my house several times a year.  I guess I have changed.)

However, I am very glad that Esther had a fun night with friends.  I consider that a good start of the Christmas season.  Thanks to the Lord.

It’s just strange that I could not handle the fun stuff anymore.  It is probably typical for people who are grieving.

 

God continue sending brothers and sisters and friends to comfort us through many different ways. 

God is so good.  But again, the question remains why He took Lydia away in the first place.

Another day went by, a day closer for me to get the answer, and to meet Lydia again…
