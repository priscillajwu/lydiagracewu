Subject: Walk of Heroes

 

To Esther and her family,

 

I have a new hero.

I met her at the Relay for life in May 2006.  I was so glad to meet Lydia that I quickly wanted to give her a hug and thank her for coming to the survivor reception.  I felt rather foolish that I didn't consider her need to stay away from germs.  I too spent many months with low white counts and needed to be careful.  I remember her beautiful eyes looking over her mask.  Eyes full of faith and love.

I learned of her passing just last week.  Esther approached me after the Relay meeting and gently told me of her journey.  I was shocked.  I guess I just thought I would see her again in May at the next Relay of Life. 

Esther told me she would forward Lydia's web site and I spent hours reading.  I have never felt such deep faith and love in any written words like these.  I am still in awe.

I am the co-chair of Relay for Life again this year.

In honor of Lydia I am creating a new event at the Relay.  This year will be our first Walk of Heroes.

This is an opportunity to play special music, have readings, (red balloons), and what ever else we can do to celebrate special heroes.

We will have a (weather proof) banner for each hero.

We will hang the banners at all future Relays.

In keeping with the spirit and support of the American Cancer Society, each hour long Walk of Heroes will have a $100.00 sponsor. 

My family is honored to be the sponsor of the Lydia Wu hour.  It is a small way to show how much we are moved by her faith and strength.

As the planning progresses we will have Lydia's family and friends contribute to the hour with special activities that represent her courage and faith.

I thank God for crossing paths with Lydia.  My life is richer for our meeting.

Keeping you all in my prayers,

Peace,
